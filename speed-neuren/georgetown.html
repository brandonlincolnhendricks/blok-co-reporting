Georgetown sits in Williamson County about 30 to 40 minutes north of downtown Austin along Interstate 35. It is best known for the historic Williamson County Courthouse square, often called the most beautiful town square in Texas, for value pricing relative to Austin, and for Sun City Texas, one of the largest active-adult communities in the state. The city is one of the fastest-growing in the country, and Georgetown ISD serves most of it.

Why People Choose Georgetown

Georgetown wins buyers on a combination that is hard to find together in the Austin metro: genuine historic charm, a true town center, and pricing that still leaves room in the budget.

Historic Charm With a Real Downtown

Unlike many Austin suburbs that grew out of cul-de-sacs and strip centers, Georgetown grew around a courthouse square that has been the heart of town for well over a century. The Victorian-era buildings, the local restaurants and boutiques, the seasonal festivals, and the community gatherings give Georgetown an identity that newer developments simply cannot manufacture. For a lot of our clients, that sense of place is the deciding factor.

Value Pricing

Money goes further in Georgetown. The same budget that buys a modest, older home closer to Austin can buy a larger, newer house with a real yard here. For families priced out of central Austin, for remote workers who do not need a daily downtown commute, and for retirees stretching a fixed income, that value is the headline. Texas has no state income tax, which makes the overall cost picture even more attractive for relocating households.

Fast Growth and Investment

Georgetown has been among the fastest-growing cities in the United States in recent years, which brings new retail, new dining, expanded healthcare, and continued infrastructure investment. Growth is a double-edged sword, and we are honest about that, but it also means the market here has momentum and a deepening base of amenities.

The Historic Square and Lifestyle

The courthouse square is the soul of Georgetown, and it shapes daily life in a way that is unusual for a metro suburb.

The Most Beautiful Town Square in Texas

The Williamson County Courthouse anchors a square ringed by restored historic storefronts that now house local restaurants, coffee shops, wine bars, antique and boutique retail, and galleries. It is genuinely walkable, hosts festivals and markets through the year, and lights up beautifully around the holidays. Buyers consistently tell us the square is what made them fall in love with Georgetown.

Outdoors and the San Gabriel River

Georgetown is built along the San Gabriel River, with trails, parks, and the spring-fed Blue Hole swimming spot near downtown. Lake Georgetown to the northwest adds boating, fishing, hiking, and camping, and the Hill Country is an easy drive west. The outdoor access is a real part of the lifestyle here, not an afterthought.

Everyday Convenience

Beyond the square, Georgetown has the everyday retail, grocery, and healthcare you expect, plus a regional hospital and growing medical presence. The mix of historic core and modern convenience is part of why the city retains residents across life stages.

Sun City and the 55+ Market

If there is one thing that sets Georgetown apart in the Austin metro, it is Sun City Texas, and it deserves its own section.

What Sun City Offers

Sun City Texas is one of the largest active-adult communities in the state, built for residents aged 55 and older. It spans thousands of homes across many phases and a wide range of floor plans and price points, from attainable resale homes to larger custom builds. The amenity package is extensive: multiple golf courses, recreation and fitness centers, pools, pickleball and tennis, walking trails, and hundreds of clubs and interest groups with a built-in social calendar.

Who Sun City Is For

The community is a natural fit for retirees and empty nesters relocating to the Austin area who want single-level living, lower-maintenance lots, and an active, social lifestyle without central-Austin prices. We help these buyers compare resale homes across the different phases, since age, layout, amenity proximity, and lot all affect both lifestyle fit and resale value.

Sun City buyer checklist

  • Phase and age: Newer phases offer modern layouts; established sections often have mature landscaping and value.
  • Single-level living: Confirm the floor plan fits long-term mobility needs.
  • Amenity proximity: Distance to golf, fitness centers, and the amenity hubs varies widely.
  • HOA and fees: Understand the association dues and what they cover before you commit.

Georgetown Homes and Prices in 2026

Georgetown is one of the stronger values in the Austin metro, with prices that generally sit well below central Austin and a world away from Westlake. As a broad 2026 guide, many established and newer single-family homes trade in roughly the $350,000 to $600,000 range. Entry-level and smaller homes fall below that, while larger custom homes, acreage properties, and golf-course homes reach $700,000 to well over $1 million.

Those ranges hide real variation. A home steps from the square, a new build in a master-planned community, a Sun City resale, and an acreage property west of town are four different markets. This is where Ross's appraisal background pays off: pricing a Georgetown home off a generic per-square-foot average misses the premiums for location, lot, and condition. We price off the right comparables, and we always recommend you confirm current numbers for your specific situation.

What your budget tends to buy in Georgetown (2026)

  • Under $350K: Smaller or older homes, condos and townhomes, and some Sun City resale homes.
  • $350K to $500K: The heart of the market. Established and newer single-family homes with yards.
  • $500K to $700K: Larger newer homes, sought-after master-planned communities, premium lots.
  • $700K to $1M+: Custom homes, acreage, and golf-course or river-adjacent properties.

Schools and Commute

Two practical questions decide a lot of Georgetown purchases: the schools and the drive to Austin.

Georgetown ISD

Most of the city is served by Georgetown Independent School District, a solid, well-rounded public district with several highly rated campuses and strong athletics and fine-arts programs. It is a different market from Eanes ISD in Westlake, which Niche ranked the number one district in Texas for 2026, but Georgetown ISD pairs good schools with far more attainable home prices, a tradeoff that works well for many families. Because attendance zones and individual campus ratings vary across a growing city, confirm the specific schools that serve any home before you buy.

The Commute to Austin

Georgetown is north of Austin along I-35, and the drive to downtown generally runs about 30 to 40 minutes outside peak hours, longer when I-35 congests at rush hour. Many residents do not commute all the way downtown at all; they work in Round Rock, Cedar Park, or the north tech corridor, which keeps their daily drive short. The State Highway 130 toll route helps for trips to the airport and the east side. If a short downtown commute is essential, weigh it honestly against the value Georgetown offers.

What No One Tells You About Georgetown

We would rather you hear the caveats from us than discover them after closing.

The I-35 Commute Is Real

Interstate 35 is the backbone of the north metro, and it can be slow at peak hours. If your job requires a daily downtown commute, drive it at the time you would actually travel before you commit. For remote workers and north-corridor employees, it is rarely an issue; for downtown commuters, it is the single biggest tradeoff.

Growth Brings Construction

A fast-growing city means active development, new roads, and ongoing construction in some areas. That is mostly a positive for amenities and long-term value, but it is worth understanding which neighborhoods are still building out and where future growth is planned near a home you like.

Confirm Your School Zone and Jurisdiction

As the city expands, some areas sit in different attendance zones or even outside city limits in unincorporated Williamson County, which can affect schools, utilities, and tax rates. We verify the school feeder pattern and jurisdiction on every property, because in a growing market these details move from house to house.

Lifestyle in Georgetown

Living in Georgetown means weekend mornings on the courthouse square, river trails and Blue Hole in summer, Lake Georgetown for boating and hiking, and a calmer pace than central Austin while keeping the city within reach. The square hosts festivals and a lively holiday lights tradition, Sun City keeps the active-adult community busy year-round, and Georgetown ISD athletics and arts give families a center of community life. For many of our clients, it is the combination of value, character, and breathing room that makes Georgetown home.
