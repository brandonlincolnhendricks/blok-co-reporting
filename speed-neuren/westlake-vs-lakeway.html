Westlake vs Lakeway: Which Austin Luxury Area Is Right for You? [2026]

By The Speed & Neuren Group at Compass | Updated June 2026 | The #1 Compass team in Austin and the Austin Business Journal's #1 real estate team, 2022 to 2025

Westlake or Lakeway is one of the most common questions we field from buyers relocating to Austin. We are Ross Speed and Lindsay Neuren, Austin natives and the founders of The Speed & Neuren Group at Compass, and we sell in both areas every year. They are both genuinely excellent, and they attract very different buyers for very good reasons. This guide lays out the honest comparison the way we walk our own clients through it: location and commute, schools, prices, lifestyle, and how to decide.

The short version: Westlake (West Lake Hills) is the close-in, built-out, Eanes ISD option that has historically held value. Lakeway is the waterfront, newer-home, more-space-per-dollar option on Lake Travis, with a longer commute. The rest comes down to what you value most.

The Quick Verdict

If you want the top-ranked schools in the state and the shortest possible drive to downtown, and you are comfortable paying a premium for a built-out market, Westlake is your answer. If you want more home for the money, newer construction, larger lots, lake access, and resort and golf amenities, and a 30 to 40 minute downtown commute is acceptable, Lakeway is hard to beat.

Neither is the wrong choice. We have moved families both directions, and the regret we see most often comes from buyers who optimized for price alone or commute alone without weighing the whole picture. Below is the full picture.

The one-line summary

  • Choose Westlake if: Eanes ISD, a short downtown commute, and long-term value retention top your list.
  • Choose Lakeway if: lake life, newer homes, more space per dollar, and resort amenities matter more than the commute.

Location and Commute Compared

The single biggest practical difference between these two areas is where they sit relative to downtown Austin, and what that means for your daily drive.

Westlake: Close-In Hill Country

Most of West Lake Hills is a 10 to 20 minute drive to downtown via Bee Caves Road, Loop 360 (Capital of Texas Highway), or MoPac (Loop 1) outside of peak traffic. You get Hill Country topography, wooded lots, and long views while still being minutes from the restaurants and offices of central Austin. The honest tradeoff is that Loop 360 and the Pennybacker bridge area can congest at rush hour, so the commute is short but not always fast.

Lakeway: Farther West, On the Water

Lakeway sits on Lake Travis, considerably farther west, with a typical downtown drive of 30 to 40 minutes or more depending on your exact location and traffic on RM 620 and the 71 and 290 corridors. In exchange for the distance you get a true lake town: marinas, waterfront, golf, and a more spread-out, resort-like feel. For remote workers and households that rarely commute downtown, the distance is a minor factor. For a daily downtown commuter, it is the headline consideration.

Schools: Eanes ISD vs Lake Travis ISD

For families, school district is often the deciding factor, and this is where the two areas differ most clearly.

Westlake: Eanes ISD

Westlake and West Lake Hills are served by Eanes Independent School District, which Niche ranked the number one school district in Texas and among the top seven in the nation for 2026, with all nine of its schools earning top state and national rankings. Westlake High School is consistently ranked among the top public high schools in Texas. The strength of Eanes ISD is the single biggest driver of family demand and home values in Westlake.

Lakeway: Lake Travis ISD

Lakeway is served by Lake Travis ISD, a highly regarded and well-funded district with strong academics, athletics, and newer campuses that have grown alongside the area's development. It is a genuinely excellent district that many families choose without hesitation. On the published statewide and national rankings, Eanes typically edges it out, but for most families both districts clear the bar comfortably, and the gap is narrower than the price difference between the two areas might suggest. We always recommend confirming the specific feeder pattern for any home, since boundaries can change.

Prices and What You Get

This is where Lakeway makes its strongest case. Your dollar simply goes further on the water than it does in 78746.

Westlake Pricing

The 78746 ZIP that covers Westlake is the highest-priced market in Austin, with a 2026 median sale price around $2.4 million and a West Lake Hills city median near $2.8 million. Luxury estates trade from $4 million into the $10 million-plus range, while the most attainable entry into the area, an older home on a smaller lot, generally starts around $1.3 million to $1.6 million. You are paying for schools, proximity, and scarcity in a built-out market.

Lakeway Pricing

Lakeway offers a broad range and, for comparable square footage, generally delivers more home, newer construction, and larger lots per dollar than Westlake. Entry-level homes start well below the Westlake median, the heart of the market lands in a comfortable luxury band, and waterfront and estate properties on Lake Travis can still reach the high seven figures and beyond. The headline difference is that the same budget buys noticeably more house and land in Lakeway.

Pricing either area correctly takes the right comparables, not a generic per-square-foot average. This is where Ross's appraisal background changes outcomes, especially on view lots and waterfront where two nearby homes can differ by millions.

The value tradeoff in one sentence

In Westlake you are buying schools, proximity, and a built-out market that has historically held value; in Lakeway you are buying more square footage, newer construction, and lake access for the same money, with a longer commute as the cost. We model the actual numbers for your budget before you decide.

Lifestyle: Hill Country Close-In vs Lake Life

The day-to-day feel of these two areas is different enough that many buyers know which one fits within a single visit.

Westlake: Quiet, Wooded, Minutes From the City

Living in Westlake means Lake Austin afternoons, Barton Creek greenbelt trails, and a 15 minute drive to the restaurants and music of downtown. The area is residential and quiet by design, with shopping and dining concentrated along Bee Caves Road. Note that Lake Austin is a constant-level lake, which makes its waterfront and water access especially desirable. The rhythm of the year often runs through Eanes ISD schools and their athletics and arts.

Lakeway: A Resort-Style Lake Town

Lakeway is built around Lake Travis and a lifestyle of boating, marinas, golf, and open Hill Country. Homes tend to be newer and more spread out, with the feel of a destination community rather than a close-in suburb. The tradeoff to know is that Lake Travis is a fluctuating reservoir, so water levels and lakefront views can change with drought and rainfall, which is worth understanding before buying for the water. For households that want weekends on the lake and more room to spread out, Lakeway delivers a lifestyle Westlake cannot replicate.

Which One Is Right for You

After all the comparisons, the decision usually clarifies around three questions: How important are the schools, how much do you commute downtown, and what do you want your weekends to look like?

Choose Westlake if Eanes ISD is non-negotiable, you value being minutes from downtown, and you are comfortable paying a premium for a built-out market that has historically held value. Choose Lakeway if you want more home and land for the money, prefer newer construction, want lake access and resort amenities, and a longer commute does not change your daily life.

Our honest advice

Do not decide on price or commute alone. Visit both at the time of day you would actually drive, walk the feeder schools, and look at real comparables in your budget side by side. Frequently Asked Questions: Westlake vs Lakeway

Is Westlake or Lakeway better for families?

Both are excellent for families, and the right answer comes down to schools and commute. Westlake (West Lake Hills) is served by Eanes ISD, ranked the number one district in Texas and among the top seven nationally by Niche for 2026, with Westlake High among the top public high schools in the state, and it sits close to downtown. Lakeway is served by the highly regarded Lake Travis ISD and offers more space, newer homes, and a lake-centered lifestyle that many families love. If top schools and a short commute lead your list, lean Westlake. If yard, square footage, and lake access matter more, lean Lakeway. We help families weigh both with real comparables.

Which is more expensive, Westlake or Lakeway?

Westlake is the more expensive of the two on a like-for-like basis. The 78746 ZIP that covers Westlake is Austin's highest-priced market, with a 2026 median sale price around $2.4 million and a West Lake Hills city median near $2.8 million. Lakeway generally delivers more home, newer construction, and larger lots per dollar, with a broad price range that runs well below Westlake's median for comparable square footage, while waterfront and estate properties on Lake Travis can still reach the high seven figures and beyond. For most budgets your money goes further in Lakeway, while Westlake commands a premium for schools and proximity.

How much longer is the Lakeway commute?

Plan on roughly twice the drive time to downtown Austin. Most of Westlake is a 10 to 20 minute drive to downtown via Bee Caves Road, Loop 360, or MoPac outside peak hours, while Lakeway typically runs 30 to 40 minutes or more depending on your exact location and traffic on RM 620 and the 71 and 290 corridors. The difference is most noticeable at rush hour and on school-day mornings. For a daily downtown commuter the gap is meaningful; for remote workers or those who rarely go downtown it matters far less. Always confirm the drive from a specific home at the times you would actually travel.

Which holds value better, Westlake or Lakeway?

Westlake has historically been the stronger value-holder of the two. Its combination of top-ranked Eanes ISD, a short downtown commute, large wooded Hill Country lots, and a built-out, supply-constrained market has helped it retain value well through market cycles. Lakeway is also a strong long-term market, but its larger supply of newer construction and ongoing development mean pricing is more sensitive to what is being built nearby, and Lake Travis is a fluctuating reservoir, so a given home's value can hinge on water level and view. Neither is a sure thing, and value always comes down to the specific home, which is where appraiser-trained pricing earns its keep.
