Rollingwood is a small, affluent incorporated city in the 78746 ZIP code, wedged between West Lake Hills and Zilker Park just west of downtown Austin. It shares the top-ranked Eanes Independent School District with Westlake, but it has its own identity: smaller, flatter, more walkable, and closer to the water.

Why Rollingwood Is One of Austin's Most Coveted Small Enclaves

Rollingwood punches far above its size. It is barely a square mile, home to roughly 1,400 people, yet it sits on one of the most desirable pieces of geography in Central Texas. The combination of features below is what makes it so hard to get into.

A Walkable Village Next to the City

Most of the Eanes ISD area is Hill Country: wooded, hilly, and built around the car. Rollingwood is the exception. Its streets are flatter and leafier, the scale is more intimate, and you can genuinely walk or bike to Zilker Park, Barton Springs Pool, and the Barton Creek Greenbelt. That walkable, almost small-town character right at the edge of downtown is the single thing buyers fall in love with here.

Its Own Small City

Rollingwood is a separate incorporated city, with its own city hall, police department, and the well-loved Rollingwood community pool and park at the center of neighborhood life. Residents value the local control and the close-knit feel, where city council meetings and the summer pool season are real community touchstones.

Eanes Schools and No State Income Tax

Rollingwood sits inside Eanes ISD, the top-ranked district in Texas, which anchors family demand just as it does in Westlake. Texas also has no state income tax, so high earners can direct more of their income toward housing. Both forces feed the premium prices in this tiny market.

Very Limited Supply

There are only a few hundred homes in the entire city, and turnover is low. When a good Rollingwood property comes to market it tends to draw real attention, which is why pricing it correctly, and recognizing value quickly as a buyer, makes the difference here.

Homes and Prices in Rollingwood

Rollingwood is one of the priciest pockets within the 78746 ZIP code, and 78746 is already the highest-priced market in Austin, with a 2026 median near $2.4 million. Because Rollingwood adds walkability and a walk-to-Zilker location to the Eanes ISD foundation, values here typically start in the low $2 millions for an older or smaller home and climb into the $4 million to $7 million range for new construction and larger lots, with the most significant properties trading higher.

Those numbers move with the lot and the street. Rollingwood has a mix of original mid-century and ranch homes on its older blocks alongside new and heavily renovated luxury builds, and that range creates wide price gaps within a few blocks. This is where Ross's appraisal background changes outcomes: pricing a Rollingwood home off a generic per-square-foot average is how sellers leave money on the table and how buyers overpay. We price off the right comparables, not the easy ones, and we recommend confirming current values for your specific situation.

What your budget tends to buy in Rollingwood (2026)

  • Low $2M range: Older or smaller original homes, often a tear-down or major-renovation candidate on a prime lot.
  • $2.5M to $4M: Updated and well-kept homes on good Rollingwood streets, the heart of the market.
  • $4M to $7M: New construction and larger lots, premium blocks closest to the park and greenbelt.
  • $7M+: Trophy new builds and the largest, most location-favored properties when they come available.

Schools (Eanes ISD)

Rollingwood is served by Eanes Independent School District, the same district that drives demand throughout the Westlake area. For 2026, Niche ranked Eanes the number one school district in Texas and among the top seven in the nation, with all nine of its schools earning top state and national honors. Westlake High School is regularly named among the best comprehensive public high schools in the state.

Many Rollingwood families attend Eanes Elementary, which sits close to the city, before moving up through the Eanes feeder pattern to Hill Country Middle School and Westlake High. Because school assignment can affect a home's value, and because feeder boundaries occasionally shift, we always confirm the exact campus a specific Rollingwood address feeds into before a client buys.

Location: Walk to Zilker and Barton Springs, Minutes to Downtown

Location is Rollingwood's superpower. The city borders Zilker Park, Barton Springs Pool, and the Barton Creek Greenbelt, and for many homes those amenities are a short walk or bike ride rather than a drive. Few places in Austin let you live in a top school district and still stroll to a spring-fed swimming hole.

Downtown Austin is typically a 5 to 15 minute drive depending on traffic, crossing the river via Lamar Boulevard or MoPac (Loop 1). Austin-Bergstrom International Airport is roughly 20 to 30 minutes away depending on the route. The honest tradeoff is that being this close to Zilker means more event-day and weekend traffic near the park, especially during festivals, and lots closer to Barton Creek warrant a look at the FEMA flood map. For most buyers, the convenience far outweighs those caveats.

The California relocation math

A household earning $300,000 to $500,000 or more can save $15,000 to well over $40,000 a year by escaping California's 13.3 percent top state income tax, since Texas has none. Texas property taxes claw back part of that, but for high earners the net still favors Texas, and once you add the housing arbitrage, where California sale equity buys more home in Austin, the first-year financial swing can be substantial. Rollingwood's small inventory means relocating buyers should be ready to move quickly when the right home appears. We model the full picture for every relocation client, not just the headline tax rate.

Who Buys in Rollingwood

Rollingwood draws a distinct kind of buyer. The common thread is wanting it all in one address: top schools, a walkable lifestyle, and the shortest possible commute to the city.

Downtown Professionals and Executives

People who work downtown or value being minutes from it love that Rollingwood lets them live in Eanes ISD without a long Hill Country drive. Relocating executives from California, the Pacific Northwest, and the East Coast frequently land here when walkability and proximity matter most.

Families Who Want to Walk to the Park

For families, the pull is the rare mix of Eanes schools, the Rollingwood community pool, and being able to walk or bike to Zilker and Barton Springs. The flatter streets and village scale make day-to-day life with kids easier than in the steeper parts of the area.

Buyers Building or Renovating

Because Rollingwood has older homes on prime lots, a meaningful share of buyers come to build new or renovate substantially. Land value drives many transactions here, and understanding what a finished home will be worth on a given lot is exactly the kind of analysis Ross's appraisal background supports.

Lifestyle in Rollingwood

Living in Rollingwood means morning swims at Barton Springs, greenbelt trail runs, and a quick drive or bike to the restaurants and music of downtown and South Lamar. The city itself is residential and quiet, centered on its park and pool, with dining and shopping just over the line in Zilker, on South Lamar, and along Bee Caves Road. For families, the year often runs through Eanes ISD activities and the Rollingwood pool season, which together form a genuine center of community life in this small city.

Frequently Asked Questions About Living in Rollingwood

What is it like to live in Rollingwood?

Living in Rollingwood feels like having a quiet, tree-shaded village just minutes from downtown Austin. It is a tiny incorporated city of roughly 1,400 residents tucked between West Lake Hills and Zilker Park, with its own small city hall, police, and a beloved community pool and park. The streets are leafy and low-traffic, neighbors know each other, and you can walk or bike to Zilker, Barton Springs, and the Barton Creek Greenbelt. It combines a high-end, walkable, almost small-town atmosphere with the schools of Eanes ISD and the convenience of being right next to the city, which is a rare blend in Austin.

How much do homes cost in Rollingwood?

Rollingwood is one of the priciest pockets in the 78746 ZIP code, which is already Austin's highest-priced market with a 2026 median near $2.4 million. Because of its small size, scarce inventory, and walk-to-Zilker location, Rollingwood home values typically run from the low $2 millions for an older or smaller home up through $4 million to $7 million or more for new construction and larger lots, with trophy properties trading higher. Prices move quickly when supply is this limited, so accurate, comparable-driven pricing matters. We recommend confirming current values for your specific street and lot, since a few blocks can change the number meaningfully.

What school district serves Rollingwood?

Rollingwood is served by Eanes Independent School District, the same top-ranked district as neighboring West Lake Hills and Westlake. Niche ranked Eanes the number one school district in Texas and among the top seven in the nation for 2026, with all nine of its schools earning top state and national honors, and Westlake High School consistently named among the best public high schools in Texas. Many Rollingwood children attend Eanes Elementary, which sits very close to the city. School assignment can affect home value, so it is worth confirming the exact campus a given Rollingwood address feeds into before you buy.

How close is Rollingwood to downtown Austin and Zilker?

Rollingwood is the closest of the Eanes ISD communities to the urban core. Zilker Park, Barton Springs Pool, and the Barton Creek Greenbelt sit right at its edge, often within a short walk or bike ride. Downtown Austin is typically a 5 to 15 minute drive depending on traffic, crossing the river by way of Lamar or MoPac (Loop 1). That proximity to Zilker, Barton Springs, and downtown, combined with Eanes schools, is the single biggest reason buyers pay a premium to be in Rollingwood rather than farther out in the hills.

What is the difference between Rollingwood and West Lake Hills?

Rollingwood and West Lake Hills are two separate incorporated cities inside the same 78746 ZIP code, and both are served by Eanes ISD. Rollingwood is much smaller and sits closer to downtown and Zilker, with a flatter, more walkable, village-like feel and tighter lots. West Lake Hills is larger, deeper into the Hill Country, with bigger and often steeper wooded lots, longer views, and a more spread-out layout. Each city has its own government, services, and tax rate. Buyers who want walkability and the shortest commute lean Rollingwood; those who want more land and Hill Country privacy lean West Lake Hills.
