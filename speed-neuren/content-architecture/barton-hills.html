Barton Hills sits in central Austin in the 78704 ZIP code, immediately south and west of Zilker Park, Barton Springs Pool, and the Barton Creek Greenbelt. It is a leafy, walkable neighborhood of mid-century homes that has held its desirability for decades and continues to appreciate strongly.

Why Barton Hills Is a Central Austin Favorite

Barton Hills earns its reputation honestly. It pairs a genuinely central location with direct access to Austin's most beloved green space, and it does it with a low-key, neighborly feel that newer parts of the city cannot manufacture.

Location You Cannot Recreate

You are minutes from downtown, South Lamar, and South Congress, but the streets feel residential and quiet. That combination of being central and being calm is rare, and it is the first thing buyers notice when they spend a day here.

An Outdoor Lifestyle at the Doorstep

Zilker Park, Barton Springs Pool, and the greenbelt trailheads are all within walking or biking distance from much of the neighborhood. For a lot of residents, the day starts with a swim or a trail run, and that everyday access to nature is the heart of the appeal.

Real Walkability

Sidewalks, shaded streets, and short distances to parks, coffee, and food make Barton Hills one of the genuinely walkable neighborhoods in Austin. You can run errands, meet a neighbor, or get to the pool on foot, which is not something most of the metro can offer.

Strong, Steady Appreciation

Because the location and green-space access cannot be replicated and the neighborhood is essentially built out, Barton Hills homes have historically appreciated well and held value through market cycles. Scarcity of close-in land near Zilker is a structural support for prices.

Barton Springs, Zilker, and the Greenbelt at Your Doorstep

More than any single home feature, the green space is what people are buying in Barton Hills. The neighborhood wraps around the south and west edges of Austin's outdoor crown jewels.

Barton Springs Pool

The spring-fed pool stays around 68 to 70 degrees year-round and is a true Austin institution. Living in Barton Hills means it can be a daily ritual rather than a weekend outing, and that proximity is a lifestyle perk you simply cannot get in most neighborhoods.

Zilker Park

Zilker is the city's signature park, home to open lawns, the botanical garden, and major events through the year. Having it as an extension of the neighborhood gives families and dog owners hundreds of acres of green space a short walk away.

The Barton Creek Greenbelt

Greenbelt trailheads near the neighborhood open onto miles of hiking, limestone bluffs, and seasonal swimming holes. For trail runners, climbers, and anyone who wants to disappear into the woods for an hour, the greenbelt is the backyard.

What the green space means day to day

A typical Barton Hills weekend can mean a morning swim at Barton Springs, a midday walk through Zilker, and an afternoon on the greenbelt, all on foot or by bike. Buyers who value an active, outdoor-centered life consistently tell us this access is worth more to them than extra square footage or a bigger lot farther out. It is the neighborhood's defining advantage.

Homes and Prices in Barton Hills

Barton Hills was largely developed in the mid-twentieth century, and that mid-century character is a big part of the charm. The original housing stock is full of single-story ranch and modern homes on tree-shaded lots, many of which have been thoughtfully renovated, while others are still waiting for their update or have been replaced by new contemporary builds.

Mid-Century Character

Expect a mix: original mid-century ranches with good bones, tasteful remodels that keep the period feel, and modern new construction that maximizes the lot. The variety is part of the appeal, and it means the right home for your budget and taste is usually findable if you know how to read the comparables.

What Prices Look Like in 2026

As a close-in neighborhood beside Zilker, Barton Hills carries a location premium. As a general 2026 guide, original or lightly updated mid-century homes often start in roughly the $900,000 to $1.3 million range, well-renovated homes commonly run from about $1.3 million to $2 million, and new builds or large remodels on prime streets can reach $2.5 million or more. These are ranges, not guarantees, so confirm pricing for your specific situation.

Those ranges hide real variation. Proximity to the greenbelt, lot size and slope, the quality of a renovation, and the specific street can move value substantially between two homes a block apart. Schools in Barton Hills (Austin ISD)

An important point for families: Barton Hills is in Austin Independent School District, not Eanes ISD. It is a common mix-up because the nearby Westlake area is Eanes, but Barton Hills sits inside the city and is served by AISD campuses.

The neighborhood's home schools are typically Barton Hills Elementary, O. Henry Middle School, and Austin High School. Barton Hills Elementary is a popular, walkable neighborhood school that families specifically seek out, and the ability to walk children to class is part of the area's draw.

Verify the campus for your specific address

Austin ISD attendance boundaries can be adjusted over time, and some Barton Hills streets sit near zone edges. Before you make an offer, confirm the exact elementary, middle, and high school assignment for the specific address with the district. Who Buys in Barton Hills

The buyer pool here is distinct, and it tells you a lot about the neighborhood's identity.

Outdoor-Oriented Professionals and Families

People who run, swim, bike, and hike gravitate to Barton Hills because the green space is right there. Families who want a walkable neighborhood school and weekend trail access are a core group.

Buyers Who Prize Location Over Lot Size

Many Barton Hills buyers are deliberately trading the larger lots and newer construction of the suburbs for a central location and immediate park access. They want to be close-in, not spread out.

Design Lovers and Renovators

The mid-century housing stock attracts buyers who appreciate the architecture and want to restore or thoughtfully modernize a period home, as well as those drawn to the new contemporary builds replacing tear-downs.

Long-Term Holders

Because of steady appreciation and the irreplaceable location, Barton Hills also draws equity-minded buyers who view a home here as a durable long-term hold in one of Austin's most consistently desirable pockets.

Lifestyle in Barton Hills

Day to day, Barton Hills is about easy access to the things that make Austin Austin. Mornings can start with a swim at Barton Springs or a run on the greenbelt. The food and music of South Lamar and South Congress are a few minutes away, and downtown is a short drive or bike ride. The neighborhood itself stays quiet and green, with mature trees, friendly streets, and a strong sense of community that often centers on Barton Hills Elementary and the parks. It is a place where the outdoors is part of ordinary life, not a weekend destination.

Living in Barton Hills feels like having Zilker Park and Barton Springs as an extension of your backyard. How close is Barton Hills to Barton Springs and Zilker?

Very close, which is the whole point of the neighborhood. Barton Hills sits immediately south and west of Zilker Park and Barton Springs Pool, and from much of the neighborhood you can walk or bike to both in a few minutes. The Barton Creek Greenbelt trailheads are equally accessible, putting miles of hiking, swimming holes, and limestone bluffs at your doorstep. What is the difference between Barton Hills and Zilker?

Barton Hills and Zilker are adjacent central Austin neighborhoods in 78704 that share access to Zilker Park, Barton Springs, and the greenbelt, and the names are sometimes used loosely. In practice, Barton Hills sits to the south and west, climbing into the hills near the greenbelt with a quieter, more residential feel and strong ties to Barton Hills Elementary. The Zilker neighborhood sits closer to Lady Bird Lake and South Lamar, slightly more urban and central. Both are walkable and highly desirable, with similar mid-century roots, so the right fit usually comes down to specific streets, school assignment, and how close to the park you want to be.
