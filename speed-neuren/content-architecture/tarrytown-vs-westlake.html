This is one of the most common decisions we help relocating and trade-up buyers work through: do you want central, historic, walkable Tarrytown, or the larger lots, longer views, and top-ranked schools of Westlake? The Quick Verdict

If you want to be able to walk to a coffee shop, drop the kids at a neighborhood school, stroll to Lake Austin, and be five minutes from downtown, Tarrytown is usually the answer. If you want a larger wooded lot, more square footage for the money, Hill Country privacy and views, and the number one school district in Texas, Westlake usually wins.

Tarrytown is central Austin character: 78703, Austin ISD, historic and mid-century homes, mature trees, and a walkable, established feel right on the edge of Lake Austin. Westlake is the Hill Country estate experience: 78746, Eanes ISD, bigger lots, newer and larger homes, and a more drive-everywhere rhythm in exchange for space and schools. Neither is objectively better; the right pick depends on how you actually want to live.

The short version

  • Choose Tarrytown if: walkability, central location, historic charm, and being minutes from downtown matter most.
  • Choose Westlake if: top-ranked Eanes ISD, larger lots, Hill Country views, and more home per dollar matter most.
  • Both deliver: luxury inventory, strong long-term value, Lake Austin proximity, and easy access to the city.

Central vs Hill Country Location

The clearest difference between these two areas is where they sit and how that shapes daily life.

Tarrytown: Central Austin, on the Lake

Tarrytown is a close-in neighborhood in the 78703 ZIP code, tucked between downtown Austin and Lake Austin. It has a true central-Austin grid of tree-canopied streets, with Lake Austin to the west, Lady Bird Lake and the hike-and-bike trail to the south, and downtown just a few minutes east. You feel like you are in the heart of the city because you are, while still enjoying an established, leafy, residential calm.

Westlake: Hill Country, West of Downtown

Westlake sits in the hills farther west, in the 78746 ZIP code, with a landscape of wooded ridges, winding roads, and long views. Most of Westlake is a 10 to 20 minute drive from downtown outside of peak traffic, so it is still convenient, but it trades the central grid for space and topography. Lake Austin and Barton Creek are nearby, and the overall feel is private, green, and spread out rather than walkable and dense.

Schools: Austin ISD vs Eanes ISD

For families, school district is often the single biggest factor, and this is where the two areas diverge most.

Westlake and Eanes ISD

Westlake is served by Eanes Independent School District, which Niche ranked the number one school district in Texas and number seven in the nation for 2026, with all nine of its schools earning top state and national honors. Westlake High School is consistently among the top public high schools in Texas. For buyers choosing primarily on public-school ranking, this is a powerful draw and a major support for Westlake home values.

Tarrytown and Austin ISD

Tarrytown is part of Austin Independent School District, a large urban district that includes a number of strong, well-regarded campuses serving the neighborhood. Austin ISD is more variable across the city than a small, uniformly high-performing district like Eanes, so families in Tarrytown often weigh specific campus assignments and also consider the area's well-known private schools. The honest takeaway: if district-wide ranking is the priority, Westlake leads; Tarrytown families typically build a school plan around specific campuses or private options. Always confirm current attendance zones for a specific address before you buy.

Home Styles and Prices

The housing stock in these two areas looks and feels different, and so does what your budget buys.

Tarrytown Homes

Tarrytown is known for character: historic homes, charming bungalows and Tudors, gracious mid-century houses, and a growing number of high-end renovations and modern new builds slotted onto established lots. Lots are generally smaller and more urban than Westlake's, and the premium you pay is for location, walkability, and that central-Austin pedigree. Prices commonly run from roughly the $1.5 million range into the multimillions, and prime Lake Austin frontage can reach the very top of the market.

Westlake Homes

Westlake skews toward larger homes on larger, often wooded and sloped lots, with more newer construction and sprawling estates than Tarrytown. The 78746 market is the highest-priced in Austin, with a 2026 median around $2.4 million and the city of West Lake Hills near $2.8 million; estates trade from $4 million well past $10 million. On a like-for-like basis you often get more square footage and more land for the dollar in Westlake than in central Tarrytown.

Two homes with similar asking prices in these neighborhoods can represent very different value once you account for lot, condition, school feeder, and lake or view premiums. This is where Ross's appraisal background changes outcomes, because pricing either market off a generic per-square-foot average is how buyers overpay and sellers leave money behind. We price off the right comparables, and we are happy to confirm the numbers for your specific situation.

Lifestyle and Walkability

If walkability matters to you, this section may decide the whole question.

Tarrytown: Walk to Almost Everything

Tarrytown is one of the more walkable luxury pockets in Austin. The neighborhood grid puts local shops, cafes, schools, parks, and Lake Austin within walking or short biking distance, and you are minutes from downtown, the Lady Bird Lake trail, Mayfield Park, and the cultural life of the city. It is the kind of place where you can leave the car in the garage on a Saturday and still fill the day on foot.

Westlake: Space and Privacy, by Car

Westlake's appeal is the opposite trade. The Hill Country terrain, larger lots, and winding roads mean most errands are by car, and the lifestyle centers on private outdoor space, views, greenbelt access, and the schools rather than a walkable village. Shopping and dining cluster along Bee Caves Road, with a short drive to downtown. Buyers who want quiet, room to spread out, and Hill Country privacy gladly give up the everyday walkability that defines Tarrytown.

Frequently Asked Questions: Tarrytown vs Westlake

What is the difference between Tarrytown and Westlake?

Tarrytown is a central Austin neighborhood inside the 78703 ZIP code, just west of downtown along Lake Austin, known for historic and mid-century homes, tree-lined streets, walkability, and Austin ISD schools. Westlake, the informal name for the area around West Lake Hills in 78746, sits in the Hill Country farther west, with larger wooded lots, newer and larger estates, longer views, and the top-ranked Eanes ISD. In short, Tarrytown is central, historic, and walkable; Westlake is more spacious, estate-oriented, and school-driven. Both are premium Austin markets, and the right fit depends on lifestyle priorities.

Which has better schools, Tarrytown or Westlake?

If school rankings are the deciding factor, Westlake has the edge. Westlake sits in Eanes ISD, which Niche ranked the number one school district in Texas and number seven nationally for 2026, with all of its schools top-ranked and Westlake High among the top public high schools in Texas. Tarrytown is served by Austin ISD, a large urban district with strong individual schools, including well-regarded campuses serving the area, but it does not carry the same district-wide ranking as Eanes. Families choosing primarily on public-school ranking often lean Westlake, while Tarrytown families may also weigh nearby private schools. Confirm current campus assignments for any specific address.

Which is more walkable?

Tarrytown is the more walkable of the two by a wide margin. Its central grid of tree-lined streets puts neighborhood shops, cafes, schools, parks, and Lake Austin within walking or short biking distance, and it sits minutes from downtown, the Lady Bird Lake trail, and Mayfield Park. Westlake is built around the Hill Country, with larger lots, steeper terrain, and winding roads, so most daily errands there are by car. Buyers who value being able to walk to a coffee shop or the lake usually prefer Tarrytown, while Westlake buyers trade walkability for space, privacy, and views.

Which is more expensive?

Both are among Austin's priciest areas, and they overlap heavily. The 78746 Westlake market is Austin's highest-priced overall, with a 2026 median around $2.4 million and the city of West Lake Hills near $2.8 million, and estates running well past $10 million. Tarrytown in 78703 is also a luxury market, with many homes from roughly the $1.5 million range into the multimillions, and prime Lake Austin frontage that can rival or exceed Westlake's top end. On a like-for-like basis Westlake often offers more square footage and lot for the money, while Tarrytown commands a premium for central location and walkability. Pricing should always be checked against current comparables for a specific home.
