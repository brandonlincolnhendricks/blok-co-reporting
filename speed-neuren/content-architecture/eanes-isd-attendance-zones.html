Eanes ISD Attendance Zones Explained: How They Affect Westlake Home Values

By The Speed & Neuren Group at Compass | Updated June 2026 | The #1 Compass team in Austin and the Austin Business Journal's #1 real estate team, 2022 to 2025

One of the first questions we hear from families relocating to Westlake is some version of "which school will my kids actually go to if we buy this house?" It is a smart question, because in Eanes ISD the answer is set by the home address, not by choice. We are Ross Speed and Lindsay Neuren, Austin natives and the founders of The Speed & Neuren Group at Compass, and we walk buyers through attendance zones on nearly every Westlake search.

This article explains how Eanes ISD attendance zones work, which elementary feeders exist, how middle and high school assignment flows from there, and how all of that can affect what a home is worth. One note up front: boundaries are set by the district and can change, so the zone for any specific address should always be verified with Eanes ISD directly before you rely on it.

Why Attendance Zones Matter in Westlake

In most of Westlake, the school district is the single biggest reason buyers are willing to pay a premium. For 2026, Niche ranked Eanes ISD the number one school district in Texas and seventh in the nation, with all of its schools top-ranked and Westlake High School among the top public high schools in the state. When the schools are the headline reason for the move, knowing exactly which campus a home feeds into is not a detail. It is central to the decision.

Attendance zones matter because Eanes assigns students by address. Buy on one street and your child attends one elementary; buy a few blocks away and it may be a different campus, a different community of families, and a different daily commute to drop-off. Inside Eanes every campus is excellent, so this is rarely about avoiding a weak school. It is about matching a family to the feeder that fits their life, and understanding how that preference shows up in pricing.

The Eanes ISD Elementary Feeders

Eanes ISD has five elementary schools, and this is where attendance zones get the most granular. Each elementary draws from its own set of streets and neighborhoods across the Westlake area.

The Five Elementary Campuses

The district's five elementary feeders are Eanes Elementary, Bridge Point Elementary, Cedar Creek Elementary, Forest Trail Elementary, and Barton Creek Elementary. Each serves a distinct geographic slice of the district, and the boundaries between them follow specific streets rather than neighborhood names, which is exactly why an address-level check matters.

Why we never rely on the listing portal alone

The school shown on a real estate listing site is a convenience field, and it can lag a boundary change or simply be wrong, especially for a home near a zone line. We confirm the current campus assignment with Eanes ISD for every address, so the school you are counting on is the school you actually get.

Middle and High School Assignment

Above the elementary level, the map simplifies. The five elementary feeders flow into two middle schools, and both middle schools flow into a single high school.

Two Middle Schools

Eanes ISD has two middle schools: Hill Country Middle School and West Ridge Middle School. Each elementary feeds into one of the two, so the middle school a child attends is determined by which elementary zone the home sits in. Both are top-ranked, and the practical differences families weigh are usually location, commute, and the feeder community their child will move up with rather than any gap in quality.

One Comprehensive High School

Both middle schools feed Westlake High School, the district's single comprehensive high school. That is part of what makes Eanes distinctive: no matter which elementary or middle zone a Westlake home is in, the high school destination is the same. Westlake High is consistently ranked among the top public high schools in Texas, which is one reason the whole district carries a value premium rather than just a handful of streets.

How the feeder pattern flows

  • Five elementary feeders: Eanes, Bridge Point, Cedar Creek, Forest Trail, Barton Creek.
  • Two middle schools: Hill Country Middle School and West Ridge Middle School.
  • One high school: Westlake High School, serving the entire district.
  • Always confirm: the elementary-to-middle pairing for a specific street with Eanes ISD, since assignments can be adjusted.

How Zoning Affects Home Value

Here is the honest version, because it is more nuanced than a simple "this feeder is worth more" claim. In a district where every campus is top-ranked, you do not see the dramatic value cliffs that exist where one neighborhood school is strong and the next is weak. The floor under Eanes home values is the district as a whole, not any single campus.

What still moves value is buyer demand at the street level. Families frequently target a specific elementary for proximity, for the chance to walk to school, or because friends and community are already centered there. When more buyers want a particular feeder, that competition can support pricing on those streets. Two comparable homes a short distance apart can draw different buyer pools and sell at different speeds based on which campus they feed.

This is exactly where Ross's appraisal background changes outcomes. Pricing a Westlake home off a generic per-square-foot average ignores the feeder dynamic and either leaves a seller's money on the table or has a buyer overpaying. We price off the right comparables, including homes in the same feeder zone, not just the easy ones.

The bigger picture behind the premium

Eanes ISD sits inside the 78746 ZIP code, Austin's highest-priced market, with a 2026 median around $2.4 million and a West Lake Hills city median near $2.8 million. Texas has no state income tax, which lets high earners direct more income toward housing in zones like these. Combined effective property tax in the Westlake area runs roughly 1.7 to 2.0 percent, with the Travis County FY2026 portion about 0.3758 per $100. The schools are the engine; the feeder zone is the fine-tuning.

Frequently Asked Questions About Eanes ISD Attendance Zones

How do Eanes ISD attendance zones work?

Eanes ISD assigns students to schools based on the home address, not on choice. The district draws attendance boundaries that map each street to one elementary campus, then to one of the two middle schools, and finally to a single high school. Because the district has five elementary feeders but only one comprehensive high school, the practical differences show up mostly at the elementary and middle levels. Boundaries are set by the district and can be adjusted over time for capacity, so the zone tied to any given address should be confirmed with Eanes ISD directly before you rely on it. We verify the current assignment for every client.

Does the elementary feeder affect home value in Westlake?

It can, though more subtly than people expect. Every Eanes ISD elementary is top-ranked, so there are no weak campuses to avoid, which compresses the spread you see in other metros. What still moves value is buyer preference: families often favor a specific elementary for proximity, community fit, or the walk-to-school feel of a particular neighborhood, and that demand can support pricing on those streets. Two similar homes a few blocks apart can attract different buyer pools based on the feeder. Because the effect is real but nuanced, it pays to price with local comparables rather than a blanket per-square-foot number.
