Why School District Drives Austin Home Values

In the Austin metro, the school district a home sits in is one of the single strongest drivers of its value. Two similar houses a few miles apart can differ by hundreds of thousands of dollars based on nothing more than which district and campus they feed. Families pay for assignment, and that demand flows straight into prices.

This matters even if you do not have school-age children. A top-rated district supports resale value, keeps demand steady through market cycles, and tends to attract the kind of long-term, equity-rich buyers who hold homes for years. When you buy into a strong district, you are buying a durable demand floor under your home.

Texas also has no state income tax, which lets high earners direct more of their income toward housing. That dynamic concentrates buying power in the most desirable districts and is part of why the top end of the Eanes market stays so competitive. The flip side is higher property taxes, which we cover below, but the headline is simple: in Austin, district drives price.

Eanes ISD: The Top of the Market

Eanes ISD is the most prestigious district in the Austin area and, by the most widely cited rankings, in the state. For 2026, Niche ranked Eanes the number one school district in Texas and among the top seven in the nation, with all nine of its schools earning top state and national honors. Westlake High School is consistently named the best comprehensive public high school in the Austin area and in Texas.

What Eanes ISD Serves

Eanes covers the area generally known as Westlake, immediately west of downtown Austin inside the 78746 ZIP code. That includes the cities of West Lake Hills and Rollingwood, along with enclaves such as Davenport Ranch, Rob Roy, Seven Oaks, Lost Creek, Westlake Highlands, and Cuernavaca. These are established, mostly built-out neighborhoods on wooded Hill Country lots, just 10 to 20 minutes from downtown.

The Eanes Tradeoff

The district is the smallest of the three by enrollment and the most expensive by far. There is very little new construction, inventory is limited, and you pay a premium for both the schools and the location. For families who put top-ranked schools and a short downtown commute first, that premium is usually worth it, and Eanes homes have historically held value well.

Lake Travis ISD: Lake Life and Strong Schools

Lake Travis ISD is a highly ranked district that pairs strong academics with a waterfront and resort lifestyle. It is a sought-after destination for families who want excellent schools without the Eanes price ceiling, and for buyers drawn to the lake.

What Lake Travis ISD Serves

The district covers Lakeway, Bee Cave, and the communities around Lake Travis, west of Austin. Expect more newer construction than Eanes, larger lots for the money, golf and country club communities, and genuine lake access. The tradeoff is distance: the downtown commute typically runs 30 to 40 minutes depending on traffic and where on the lake you land.

One honest local note: Lake Travis is a reservoir that fluctuates with rainfall and drought, unlike the constant-level Lake Austin closer to town. That matters for waterfront expectations, so we always set the right expectation with lake buyers before they fall for a view.

Leander ISD: Space and Value

Leander ISD is one of the larger and well-regarded districts in the Austin metro. It is the value play of the three, offering the most home per dollar and the widest range of newer construction, which makes it a popular landing spot for growing families and first-time Austin buyers.

What Leander ISD Serves

Leander serves Cedar Park, Leander, and parts of Steiner Ranch, on the northwest side of the metro. The area has seen substantial growth, with master-planned communities, newer homes, and strong amenities. Commutes vary widely depending on where you work, but for many buyers the extra space and lower entry price more than offset a longer drive.

Home Prices by District

Here is where the district comparison becomes a budget conversation. Eanes ISD sits at the top of the Austin market, Lake Travis ISD in a strong upper-middle tier, and Leander ISD as the most attainable of the three. The exact gap shifts with the specific neighborhood, home, and timing, so treat these as orientation rather than fixed numbers and confirm current comparables for your target areas.

How prices generally stack up (2026)

  • Eanes ISD: The 78746 ZIP is the most expensive market in Austin, with a 2026 year-to-date median around $2.4 million, and the city of West Lake Hills near $2.8 million. Estates trade well into the $4 million-plus range.
  • Lake Travis ISD: Strong upper-tier pricing, typically well below Eanes, with a wide range from attainable suburban homes to lakefront luxury.
  • Leander ISD: Generally the most attainable entry of the three, with the most newer construction and the most home per dollar.

How to Choose the Right District

There is no single best answer, only the best fit for your family. The way we frame it for clients comes down to a few honest questions.

Start with Budget and Priorities

If a top-of-the-state ranking and a 15 minute downtown commute are non-negotiable and the budget supports it, Eanes is the clear pick. If you want strong schools, more house, and either lake access or newer construction, Lake Travis and Leander open up real options at meaningfully lower prices.

Factor in the Full Cost

Texas has no state income tax, but property taxes are correspondingly higher across all three districts, and the combined effective rate in the Westlake area typically runs roughly 1.7 to 2.0 percent of assessed value. On a higher-priced Eanes home, that annual number is real, so it belongs in the comparison from day one, not just the purchase price.

Confirm the Zone Before You Fall in Love

Attendance zones determine which specific campus a home feeds, and they can change. A street you assume is in one zone may not be. We confirm the exact attendance zone on every property before clients commit, so there are no surprises after closing.

Our quick read for relocating buyers

Want the top ranking and the shortest commute, with budget to match? Eanes. Want strong schools plus lake life or newer construction and a bit more room in the budget? Lake Travis. Want the most home for your money and newer construction for a growing family? Leander. We will pull current comparables in any of the three so you can compare real numbers, not just rankings.
