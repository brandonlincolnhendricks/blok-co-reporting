Austin's Major Tech Employers and Where They Sit

The first move in any relocation is geography. Austin's big tech campuses sit in four different directions from downtown, and the metro is large enough that a 15 mile difference can mean a 40 minute swing in your daily commute. Before you fall in love with a neighborhood, anchor on where you will actually be driving each morning.

Where to Live for the Tesla Gigafactory Commute

Tesla's Gigafactory is southeast of the city near Del Valle, just off State Highway 130. It is one of the more isolated of the four campuses, so the right neighborhood depends heavily on how much commute you will tolerate in exchange for schools and amenities.

Closest and most convenient

For the shortest drive, look at southeast and south Austin: East Riverside, McKinney Falls, Onion Creek, and the I-35 corridor communities of Buda and Kyle. These areas have grown quickly alongside the Gigafactory and offer newer construction at prices well below central Austin, with many homes giving you a 15 to 25 minute commute outside of peak traffic.

Schools and lifestyle tradeoff

Families who prioritize schools sometimes choose southwest Austin or the Hays CISD areas around Buda and accept a 25 to 35 minute drive. SH-130 is a toll road that usually moves well, but US-183 and parts of I-35 can back up, so we always suggest test-driving your likely route at your actual start time before committing. We are happy to map options against your specific shift.

Where to Live for Apple North Campus

Apple's main Austin campus is in the north of the city near Parmer Lane, with a large expansion campus close by. This puts the convenient neighborhoods in the north and northwest, where buyers find some of the metro's best combinations of newer homes and strong schools.

North and northwest neighborhoods

The most direct commutes come from the Domain area, Round Rock, Cedar Park, Leander, Avery Ranch, and parts of Pflugerville. Round Rock ISD and Leander ISD neighborhoods are popular with Apple families for highly ranked schools and newer construction at a friendlier price than central Austin. Leander and Cedar Park in particular have become relocation favorites for that reason.

For buyers who want top-tier schools and luxury

Some Apple employees prioritize the very top schools and an established luxury home over the shortest commute. Those buyers often land in Westlake, served by Eanes ISD, which Niche ranked the number one district in Texas and among the top seven nationally for 2026, with all of its schools top-ranked and Westlake High among the top public high schools in Texas. The reverse commute north on MoPac or Loop 360 runs roughly 30 to 40 minutes. It is a real tradeoff, and we help you weigh it honestly.

Where to Live for Oracle and Downtown

Oracle has one of the best-located campuses of the four, sitting on the south shore of Lady Bird Lake just east of downtown. Because it is so central, almost every desirable Austin neighborhood is within reasonable reach, which gives Oracle employees the widest set of choices.

Walkable and close-in

For a walkable, urban lifestyle, look at downtown and East Austin condos, South Congress, Travis Heights, and Mueller to the north. These put you minutes from the office and from the restaurants, music, and lake life that draw people to Austin in the first place.

Established luxury and top schools

Buyers who want established luxury and the top-ranked Eanes ISD often choose Westlake or Tarrytown to the west, both within a 10 to 20 minute drive of the Oracle campus outside of peak traffic. The 78746 ZIP that covers Westlake is Austin's highest-priced market, with a 2026 median around $2.4 million and the city of West Lake Hills nearer $2.8 million. Oracle's central location means you do not have to sacrifice schools or a short commute to get there.

Where to Live for Samsung in Taylor

Samsung's major semiconductor plant is in Taylor, northeast of Austin in Williamson County. It is the farthest of the four campuses from the city center, so the calculus here leans hardest toward living close in or accepting a long drive for central amenities.

Closest communities

The shortest commutes come from Taylor itself, plus Hutto and Round Rock, with Pflugerville and Georgetown within a reasonable drive. These northeast communities offer newer construction and more home for the money than central Austin, with school options in Taylor ISD, Hutto ISD, and Round Rock ISD. For many Samsung families, a newer home a short drive from the plant beats a long daily haul.

If you want central Austin or top schools

Buyers set on top-ranked Eanes ISD or central-city living can still make Samsung work, but the drive from Westlake or downtown runs 45 minutes or more each way. We are direct with clients about that: a long commute looks fine on a map and feels very different on a Tuesday in February. If the schools matter most, we help you decide whether the closer-in northeast options or the longer Westlake commute is the better fit.

Balancing Commute, Schools, and Lifestyle

The hardest part of relocating is that the perfect neighborhood rarely exists. You are almost always trading among three things: commute time, school quality, and lifestyle. The buyers who are happiest a year later are the ones who decided up front which of the three matters most.

The three levers every relocating buyer balances

  • Commute: Test-drive your actual route at your actual start time. A 15 mile difference can be a 40 minute swing in Austin traffic.
  • Schools: If schools lead, anchor on the district first, then the home. Eanes ISD, Round Rock ISD, and Leander ISD are common relocation targets.
  • Lifestyle: Walkable and urban, Hill Country and private, or newer-build suburban each pull you toward different parts of the metro.

A few patterns hold up across all four employers. Northwest communities like Leander and Cedar Park serve Apple and Samsung families well with newer homes and good schools. Westlake and Tarrytown work beautifully for Oracle and for anyone who will accept a reverse commute in exchange for the top schools in Texas. South and southeast Austin and the Buda and Kyle corridor are the natural fit for Tesla. And no matter where you land, the no state income tax advantage and the housing value relative to coastal markets are part of what makes the move pencil out.

A caveat we always share

Tech campuses expand, shift buildings, and add satellite sites over time, and traffic patterns change with them. Before you buy, confirm exactly which building or campus you will report to and drive that commute yourself. We help relocation clients pressure-test the route, the schools, and the budget together so the home fits your life, not just the offer letter.
