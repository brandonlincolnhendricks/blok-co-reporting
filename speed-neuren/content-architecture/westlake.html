West Lake Hills, commonly just called Westlake, is a cluster of affluent neighborhoods in the Hill Country immediately west of downtown Austin, inside the 78746 ZIP code. It is anchored by the top-ranked Eanes Independent School District and is consistently the highest-value residential market in the Austin metro.

Why Buyers Choose Westlake

Westlake does not lead the market by accident. A rare combination of schools, location, terrain, and tax structure creates demand that very few areas in Texas can match.

The Eanes ISD Effect

The number one reason families pay a premium for Westlake is Eanes ISD. For 2026, Niche ranked Eanes the number one school district in Texas and among the top seven in the nation, with all nine of its schools earning top state and national honors. Westlake High School is regularly named the best comprehensive public high school in the Austin area and in the state. In a market where school assignment can swing a home's value, buying inside Eanes is the foundation of the Westlake premium.

No State Income Tax

Texas has no state income tax. For a household relocating from California, where the top rate reaches 13.3 percent, or from New York, that alone can mean tens of thousands of dollars a year staying in the family's pocket. High earners can redirect that money toward a larger mortgage, which is part of why the upper end of the Westlake market stays competitive.

Ten Minutes to Downtown, Worlds Away in Feel

Most of Westlake is a 10 to 20 minute drive from downtown Austin, yet it feels like the Hill Country: wooded lots, long ridge views, winding roads, and Lake Austin and Barton Creek nearby. That blend of proximity and privacy is the second pillar of demand after the schools.

Limited Supply

Westlake is essentially built out. There is very little new construction, lots are large, and turnover is modest, so well-priced homes in strong locations move quickly. Scarcity is a structural feature of this market, not a temporary condition.

Westlake Home Prices in 2026

The 78746 ZIP code that covers Westlake is the most expensive market in Austin. The 2026 year-to-date median sale price is roughly $2.39 million, and within the city of West Lake Hills the median runs higher, near $2.8 million. Luxury estates trade from $4 million into the $10 million-plus range, while the most attainable entry into the area, an older home on a smaller lot, generally starts around $1.3 million to $1.6 million.

Those medians hide enormous variation. Two homes a quarter mile apart can differ by millions based on Lake Austin frontage, Hill Country views, lot size, school feeder, and condition. The Enclaves of Westlake

Westlake is not one neighborhood. It is a collection of enclaves, each with its own price band, character, and buyer. Knowing the differences is half of buying well here.

Davenport Ranch

Gated luxury near Lake Austin, with golf, larger estates, and some of the area's best water access. A favorite of buyers who want resort-style living minutes from downtown.

Rob Roy

A guard-gated estate community known for large lots and some of the most significant homes in Westlake. Privacy and scale define it.

Seven Oaks

A quiet, upscale pocket close to downtown, popular with buyers who want established luxury without a long drive.

Lost Creek

One of the more attainable ways into Eanes ISD, set around a golf course with a strong community feel. A frequent landing spot for first-time Westlake buyers.

Westlake Highlands and Cuernavaca

Established neighborhoods with good value, mature trees, and a range of original and updated homes. Strong options for buyers prioritizing the schools over square footage.

Rollingwood (Adjacent Sister City)

Technically its own small city between Westlake and Zilker, Rollingwood offers a walkable, high-demand character closest to downtown and Barton Springs. We cover it in its own dedicated guide.

Property Taxes and the Texas Tradeoff

The flip side of no state income tax is higher property taxes, and Westlake buyers should plan for it. The total rate combines City of West Lake Hills, Travis County, and Eanes ISD levies. Travis County's adopted FY2026 county rate is about 0.3758 per $100 of taxable value, and once the city and school district are added, the combined effective rate in the Westlake area typically lands in roughly the 1.7 to 2.0 percent range of assessed value.

On a $2.5 million home, that is a real annual number, so it belongs in your budget from day one. Two things soften it: the Texas homestead exemption on a primary residence, and the right to protest your assessed value every year. We help clients with both, and Ross's appraisal background is genuinely useful when a protest comes down to defensible comparable values.

The California relocation math

A household earning $300,000 to $500,000 or more can save $15,000 to well over $40,000 a year by escaping California's state income tax. Texas property taxes claw back part of that, but for high earners the net still favors Texas, and once you add the housing arbitrage, where California sale equity buys more home in Westlake, the first-year financial swing can exceed $50,000 to $80,000. We model the full picture for every relocation client, not just the headline tax rate.

What No One Tells You About Westlake

We would rather you hear the caveats from us than discover them after closing.

Traffic on Loop 360 and Bee Caves

The same Hill Country geography that makes Westlake beautiful funnels traffic onto a few roads. Loop 360, the Pennybacker bridge area, and Bee Caves Road can back up at rush hour. The downtown commute is short, but it is not always fast.

Hilly Lots and Older Systems

Many Westlake homes sit on steep, wooded lots, which is part of the charm and also a maintenance and renovation consideration. Some properties are on septic rather than sewer, and a few on well water, especially on larger lots, so the water and wastewater setup is always worth verifying.

Flood and Wildfire Awareness

Lots along Barton Creek, Eanes Creek, and other drainages can fall in FEMA flood zones, and the wooded terrain warrants ordinary wildfire awareness. Most homes are not high-risk, but the FEMA map and insurance picture should be checked on any specific property.

Lifestyle in Westlake

Living in Westlake means Lake Austin afternoons, Barton Creek greenbelt trails, and a 15 minute drive to the restaurants and music of downtown. The area is residential and quiet by design, with shopping and dining concentrated along Bee Caves Road and a short hop to the Westlake and downtown scenes. For families, the rhythm of the year often runs through Eanes ISD schools and their athletics and arts, which are a genuine center of community life here.

Most of West Lake Hills is 10 to 20 minutes from downtown via Bee Caves Road, Loop 360, or MoPac outside peak hours. Austin-Bergstrom airport is roughly 25 to 35 minutes away.

Yes, particularly around Davenport Ranch and the streets that drop toward the water, including homes with private docks. Lake Austin is a constant-level lake, which makes its waterfront especially desirable. Waterfront carries a significant premium.

What is the difference between West Lake Hills, Rollingwood, and Westlake?

Westlake is the informal name for the broader Eanes ISD area in 78746. West Lake Hills and Rollingwood are separate incorporated cities within it, with Rollingwood closest to downtown. There are also unincorporated Travis County pockets locals call Westlake. The jurisdiction affects services and tax rates.

Do Westlake homes have city water and sewer or septic?

It varies. Many homes are on municipal or district water and sewer, but some, especially on larger lots, are on septic and occasionally well water. Verify the setup during due diligence, as it affects maintenance and renovation.
