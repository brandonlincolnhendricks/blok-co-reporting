Is Westlake Worth the Price Premium? An Honest Answer [2026]

By The Speed & Neuren Group at Compass | Updated June 2026 | The #1 Compass team in Austin and the Austin Business Journal's #1 real estate team, 2022 to 2025

It is the question almost every serious Austin buyer asks us at some point: is Westlake actually worth what it costs? We are Ross Speed and Lindsay Neuren, Austin natives and the founders of The Speed & Neuren Group at Compass, and we have closed more than a billion dollars in Central Texas real estate, a large share of it in these hills. So we are going to give you the honest answer rather than the brochure version.

The short version: Westlake is worth the premium for some buyers and a poor value for others, and the difference comes down to one question. Are you buying the schools and the location, or are you buying the home? If it is the first, Westlake is hard to beat. If it is the second, you can get noticeably more house for your money a short drive west. Here is how we think it through with our own clients.

What You Are Actually Paying For in Westlake

Before you can decide whether the premium is worth it, you have to be clear about what the premium actually buys. In Westlake (the 78746 ZIP code west of downtown Austin), you are not paying for the most square footage. You are paying for a stack of things that rarely come together in one place.

The Real Components of the Premium

The Westlake price tag is built from school access, location, terrain, and scarcity, in roughly that order of importance to most buyers. Eanes ISD is the foundation. Proximity to downtown is the second pillar. The Hill Country setting, the wooded lots, and the Lake Austin and Barton Creek access are the third. And because the area is essentially built out, limited supply keeps steady upward pressure on prices. When you write an offer in Westlake, you are buying that bundle, not just the four walls.

What the Westlake premium actually buys

  • Schools: Eanes ISD, ranked #1 in Texas and #7 nationally by Niche for 2026, with all of its schools top-ranked.
  • Location: A 10 to 20 minute drive to downtown Austin, among the closest luxury areas to the core.
  • Setting: Hill Country topography, long views, mature trees, Lake Austin and Barton Creek access.
  • Scarcity: A built-out market with very little new inventory, so well-located homes hold demand.

The Case For the Premium

For the right buyer, the case for Westlake is genuinely strong, and we would not steer those clients anywhere else.

The Eanes ISD Advantage

The single biggest reason families pay the premium is Eanes ISD. For 2026, Niche ranked Eanes the number one school district in Texas and among the top seven in the nation, with all nine of its schools earning top state and national honors, and Westlake High School consistently ranked among the best public high schools in Texas. If you have children who will go through public school, the cost of comparable private education over a decade or more can rival a meaningful slice of the price premium itself. For these buyers, the schools are not a luxury, they are the core purchase.

Location and Lifestyle

Most of Westlake is a 10 to 20 minute drive from downtown Austin, yet it feels like the Hill Country. That blend of a short commute and genuine privacy is rare, and it is the second pillar of demand after the schools. Texas also has no state income tax, which lets high earners direct more of their income toward housing, and that structural advantage keeps the upper end of the market competitive year after year.

Value Retention

Westlake has historically held value well through market cycles. The drivers are structural rather than speculative: scarce inventory in a built-out area, durable demand anchored by the schools and the location, and the no-income-tax advantage that keeps relocating executives competing for homes. No market is guaranteed, but the combination of limited supply and steady demand has supported value retention better than many of the alternatives, which matters if you view the home as a long-term hold.

The Case Against the Premium

We owe you the other side just as plainly. For a real share of buyers, the Westlake premium does not pencil out, and pretending otherwise would not serve you.

More Home Per Dollar Elsewhere

If your priority is space, newness, and amenities rather than the Eanes schools, your dollar simply goes further west of Westlake. Lakeway and Bee Cave on Lake Travis, served by the highly ranked Lake Travis ISD, and Steiner Ranch typically deliver larger lots, newer construction, resort and golf amenities, and a lower price per square foot. The tradeoff is a longer 30 to 40 minute commute downtown and a different school district. For a buyer without school-age children, or one who would choose private school regardless, paying a Westlake premium for an older home on a hilly lot can be hard to justify.

The Honest Tradeoffs Inside Westlake

Even within Westlake, the premium does not buy a perfect product. Many homes sit on steep, wooded lots that carry real maintenance and renovation considerations. Some properties are on septic rather than sewer, and a few on well water. The same Hill Country geography that makes the area beautiful funnels traffic onto a few roads, so Loop 360 and Bee Caves Road can back up at rush hour. None of this is disqualifying, but a buyer paying top of market deserves to know that the premium is for schools, location, and scarcity, not for turnkey newness.

Who Should Pay the Premium and Who Should Not

After hundreds of these conversations, the answer usually sorts into a few clear profiles. The premium decision is rarely about the home in isolation, it is about how your life maps onto what the premium actually buys.

Who the premium tends to be worth it for

  • Families with school-age children who will attend public school and want Eanes ISD.
  • Buyers who place a high value on a short, reliable connection to downtown Austin.
  • Long-term holders who prioritize value retention and scarcity over square footage.
  • Relocating high earners using the no-income-tax advantage and housing arbitrage to upgrade location.

Who should think twice before paying it

  • Buyers without school-age children, or who plan on private school regardless.
  • Buyers whose top priority is maximum square footage, new construction, or lake and resort amenities.
  • Anyone comfortable with a longer downtown commute in exchange for more home per dollar.
  • Shorter-term buyers who may not hold long enough to benefit from value retention.

If you see yourself in the first group, Westlake is very likely worth it, and we would put the data behind that decision. If you see yourself in the second, we would rather show you Lakeway, Bee Cave, or Steiner Ranch and help you buy more home than push you into a premium you will not fully use.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Westlake worth the higher price?

For buyers who genuinely value the top-ranked Eanes ISD schools and a 10 to 20 minute downtown commute, Westlake is usually worth the premium, and homes there have historically held value well through market cycles. For buyers who care more about square footage, new construction, or lake amenities, the premium is harder to justify because Lakeway, Bee Cave, and Steiner Ranch deliver more home per dollar. The honest answer is that it depends on whether you are buying the schools and location or the home itself. We walk every client through that tradeoff with real comparables before they commit.

What do you get for the Westlake premium?

The Westlake premium buys a rare stack of things that almost never appear together: the top-ranked Eanes Independent School District, which Niche ranked number one in Texas and number seven nationally for 2026, a short 10 to 20 minute drive to downtown Austin, Hill Country topography with long views and mature trees, Lake Austin and Barton Creek access, large wooded lots, and very limited supply because the area is essentially built out. You are paying for scarcity, schools, and location rather than for the most square footage, which is the key distinction buyers should understand before they offer.

The drivers are structural: the top-ranked Eanes ISD, limited new inventory in a built-out area, proximity to downtown, and Texas having no state income tax, which keeps high earners competing for homes. No market is guaranteed, but the combination of scarce supply and durable demand has supported value retention better than many alternatives.
