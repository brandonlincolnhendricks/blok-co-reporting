Downtown Condo vs Westlake Estate: Two Austin Luxury Lifestyles

By The Speed & Neuren Group at Compass | Updated June 2026 | The #1 Compass team in Austin and the Austin Business Journal's #1 real estate team, 2022 to 2025

One of the most interesting conversations we have with Austin luxury buyers is not about a single home, it is about a fork in the road. Do you want a sleek downtown condo with the city at your doorstep, or a Westlake estate with space, privacy, and the top-ranked schools? We are Ross Speed and Lindsay Neuren, Austin natives and the founders of The Speed & Neuren Group at Compass, and we have closed more than a billion dollars in Central Texas real estate on both sides of this decision.

These are two very different products bought by two very different buyers, and the right answer has almost nothing to do with which one is objectively better. It has everything to do with how you live. Here is the honest, side-by-side breakdown we give our own clients.

Two Very Different Luxury Lifestyles

A downtown condo and a Westlake estate both sit at the top of the Austin market, but they solve for opposite priorities. The condo is about being in the middle of everything with as little maintenance as possible. The estate is about creating your own world: room to spread out, mature trees, and a school district worth relocating for.

Think of it as urban convenience versus space and roots. Neither is a compromise. They are simply built for different lives, and many of our clients fit one profile cleanly while a few want a foot in each. The first step is being honest about which version of luxury you actually want to live in day to day.

How the Two Buyers Differ

Condo buyers tend to be travelers, empty nesters, single professionals, second-home owners, and investors who prize walkability and a lock-and-leave footprint. Estate buyers tend to be growing families, executives relocating for the schools, and equity-rich owners who want privacy and land. The same person can move from one to the other as life changes, which is why we treat this as a lifestyle question first and a real estate question second.

The Downtown Condo Case

The strongest argument for a downtown Austin condo is freedom. Lock the door, head to the airport, and there is no lawn to mow, no pool to service, and no roof to worry about. For frequent travelers and second-home owners, that lock-and-leave simplicity is the entire point.

Then there is walkability. From the right tower you can walk to dinner on Rainey Street, a show downtown, the trail around Lady Bird Lake, and in many cases your office. Luxury condo buildings layer on amenities most single-family homes cannot match: concierge, fitness centers, pools, secured parking, and event spaces, all maintained for you.

Who the downtown condo fits

  • Lock-and-leave owners: frequent travelers and second-home buyers who want zero exterior maintenance.
  • Urban professionals: those who value walking to work, dining, and the lake.
  • Empty nesters: downsizers trading yard work for amenities and a view.
  • Investors: buyers seeking a rentable, liquid asset in a deep urban demand pool.

The Honest Tradeoffs

Condos come with monthly HOA dues you do not control, shared walls and elevators, and far less private outdoor space. Your value is partly tied to the building's management, its reserves, and how much new inventory comes online nearby. These are not dealbreakers, but they are real, and you should weigh them before you fall for the view.

The Westlake Estate Case

The case for a Westlake estate starts with two things a condo cannot give you: space and schools. Westlake, the area in and around West Lake Hills inside the 78746 ZIP code, is built on large wooded lots in the Hill Country just west of downtown, and it is anchored by Eanes Independent School District.

For 2026, Niche ranked Eanes the number one school district in Texas and among the top seven in the nation, with all nine of its schools earning top honors and Westlake High School consistently named among the best public high schools in the state. For families, that single fact often settles the decision. Add privacy, room for kids and pets, and the option of Lake Austin or Barton Creek access, and the estate becomes a long-term home rather than a base.

Who the Westlake estate fits

  • Families: buyers who want the top-ranked Eanes ISD and room to grow.
  • Privacy seekers: owners who value land, trees, and Hill Country quiet.
  • Relocating executives: households trading California or coastal prices for space and no state income tax.
  • Long-term holders: buyers in a built-out, supply-constrained market that has historically held value.

The Honest Tradeoffs

An estate is a bigger commitment in every sense: more square footage to maintain, larger property tax bills, a car-dependent lifestyle, and a downtown commute that, while short at 10 to 20 minutes, is not walkable. The freedom of the condo is replaced by the responsibility of a home. For the right buyer that responsibility is exactly what they want.

Costs Compared: HOA vs Property Tax and Upkeep

Carrying cost is where these two paths diverge most, and the comparison is rarely apples to apples. A condo concentrates its costs into monthly HOA dues plus property tax on the unit. An estate spreads its costs across property tax on a much higher value, plus the real, ongoing upkeep of a large home and lot.

Texas has no state income tax, and the tradeoff statewide is higher property taxes. In the Westlake area the combined effective rate, blending city, Travis County (whose adopted FY2026 county rate is about 0.3758 per $100 of taxable value), and Eanes ISD, typically lands in the rough 1.7 to 2.0 percent range of assessed value. On a multimillion-dollar estate that is a significant annual number. A downtown condo carries property tax too, but on a lower base, with the larger swing being the HOA dues.

Investment and Resale Considerations

If you are weighing these as investments and not only as homes, the two behave differently and that is worth understanding up front.

The Downtown Condo as an Asset

Condos are more liquid and appeal to a broad, deep pool of urban buyers and renters, which makes them attractive for lock-and-leave flexibility and rental income. The flip side is that values are sensitive to how many new towers come online nearby and to the financial health of the HOA. A well-run building in a prime location holds up well; an oversupplied submarket or a building with thin reserves can lag.

The Westlake Estate as an Asset

Westlake sits in a built-out, supply-constrained market anchored by Eanes ISD, and that scarcity has historically supported value through market cycles. You are buying land and a school district as much as a structure, and those tend to be durable. Resale can take longer than a condo because the buyer pool is narrower, but the right home on the right street in 78746 rarely lacks for demand.

The honest investment caveat

Neither path wins automatically. Condos can deliver income and liquidity; estates can deliver land-and-school appreciation and a long-term hold. Past performance is not a promise, and the right answer depends on your timeline, your tax picture, and current comparables. We model both against your actual plans rather than relying on a headline number, and we recommend you confirm the latest data for your specific situation.

Which Fits Your Life

Strip away the marketing and the choice gets simple. If you want to walk out the door with nothing to maintain, be in the middle of the city, and keep your footprint light, the downtown condo is built for you. If you want space, privacy, room for a family, and the top-ranked Eanes ISD schools, the Westlake estate is built for you.

And if you find yourself drawn to both, you are not alone. A number of our clients pair a Westlake estate as the family home with a downtown condo as a lock-and-leave second place, or run it the other way around. There is no wrong answer here, only the answer that matches how you actually want to spend your weeks. That is the conversation we are happy to have with you, with real numbers on the table.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I buy a downtown Austin condo or a Westlake home?

It comes down to how you actually live. A downtown condo suits buyers who want a walkable, lock-and-leave base: travelers, empty nesters, second-home owners, and professionals who value being steps from restaurants, offices, and the lake without yard or maintenance duties. A Westlake estate suits buyers who need space, privacy, and access to the top-ranked Eanes ISD schools, and who want room for a family and Hill Country quiet. If you crave low-maintenance urban energy, lean condo. If you want square footage, land, and schools, lean Westlake. Many of our clients ultimately weigh both, and we help them decide with real numbers.

Which costs more to own over time?

Both carry meaningful carrying costs, just in different forms. A downtown condo pays monthly HOA dues that can run from a few hundred dollars to well over a thousand dollars a month in luxury towers, covering amenities, insurance on common areas, and reserves, plus property tax on the unit. A Westlake estate has no HOA in many enclaves but carries a higher combined effective property tax, typically in the rough 1.7 to 2.0 percent range of assessed value, plus real upkeep on a larger home and lot. On a high-value estate the tax and maintenance number usually exceeds condo dues. Confirm the exact figures for your specific situation before you commit.

Which is the better investment?

Neither wins automatically; they tend to perform differently. Downtown condos can offer strong rental and lock-and-leave flexibility and appeal to a deep pool of urban buyers, but values move with the supply of new towers and with HOA health. Westlake estates sit in a built-out, supply-constrained market anchored by Eanes ISD, and that scarcity has historically supported value through cycles. For pure appreciation tied to land and schools, the estate has structural advantages; for income and liquidity, the condo can be compelling. We model both against your timeline rather than guessing, and you should confirm the latest comparables for your specific situation.

Can I have both a lock-and-leave and a family home?

Yes, and a number of our clients do exactly this. A common pattern is a Westlake estate as the primary family residence inside Eanes ISD, paired with a downtown condo as a lock-and-leave pied-a-terre for nights out, game days, or visiting family, or as an investment unit. Others reverse it: a downtown condo as the everyday base and a Hill Country property as the weekend retreat. The right structure depends on where your time, your children's schools, and your tax picture point. We help clients sequence the purchases and weigh the combined carrying costs so the second property adds lifestyle, not stress.
