If you are weighing a Seattle to Austin move in 2026, this is the full picture.

Why Seattle Professionals Are Moving to Austin

The Seattle to Austin pipeline runs largely on technology. Both cities are major tech hubs, and the same employers and talent move between them. When a role opens in Austin, or remote work makes location flexible, Austin keeps coming up for a stack of reasons.

The Tech Connection

Austin is now home to large operations for the same kinds of employers that built Seattle's tech economy, plus marquee names like Tesla, Apple, Oracle, and Samsung. Many of our Seattle clients are following a job, transferring within a company, or arriving as remote workers who can finally choose their city. The professional network and the type of work feel familiar; the cost structure does not.

Housing That Stretches Further

The single biggest pull is housing. Seattle equity buys more home in Austin: more square footage, a newer build, a better lot, or a shorter commute. For buyers coming out of a small Seattle bungalow or a downtown condo, the difference in what the same dollars buy can be dramatic, and it is the number that most often tips the decision.

Sunshine and Growth

After years of gray Seattle winters, the prospect of roughly 230 sunny days a year is its own draw. Layer on Austin's continued job and population growth, a deep outdoor and music culture, and a younger feel to the city, and the move starts to make sense well beyond a spreadsheet.

The Cost and Growth Case

Let's be precise about the financial story, because the Seattle math is different from the California math you may have read about.

On housing, Austin is the clear winner. Greater Austin's median home price generally runs in the $450,000 to $550,000 range, while the Seattle metro median sits notably higher, often $750,000 to $850,000, with central Seattle neighborhoods higher still. For a Seattle homeowner sitting on substantial equity, that gap is the heart of the move: the same money buys more home, or frees up cash, or shortens the commute.

Important: Washington already has no income tax

Unlike a move from California, a Seattle to Austin relocation does not unlock a big state income-tax windfall, because Washington has no state income tax either. The draw here is housing cost, cost of living, weather, and growth, not income tax. In fact, Texas property tax rates are higher than Washington's, so part of your housing savings is offset by a larger annual tax bill. We model the full carrying cost, not just the sticker price, so the comparison is honest.

Where Austin still wins for Seattle buyers is the combination: a lower median home price, a broadly comparable everyday cost of living, no state income tax preserved (so you are not trading down on that front), and a metro that continues to add jobs and people. Growth is not just a feel-good number here; it has historically supported home values over time, which matters when you are putting Seattle equity into a new market.

Where PNW Buyers Land in Austin

One of the first things we do with a Seattle client is translate the neighborhoods they love into their Austin equivalents. The PNW instinct for greenery, water, and walkability maps onto specific pockets here.

Families Chasing Top Schools

Buyers who prioritize schools land in Westlake (West Lake Hills) and the Eanes ISD area, which Niche ranked the number one district in Texas and among the top seven nationally for 2026, or in Lake Travis and Leander ISD communities farther west for more home per dollar. These are the family-first choices, and they carry a school premium worth understanding before you commit.

The Capitol Hill and Ballard Crowd

If you loved walkable, character-rich Seattle neighborhoods, central and east Austin will feel most like home: Mueller (a walkable planned community), Clarksville, Travis Heights, and East Austin all offer that mix of older homes, local cafes, and a short hop to downtown.

Relocating for a Tech Job

Those moving for Tesla, Apple, Oracle, or Samsung often choose based on the commute. The northwest corridor and the Domain, Cedar Park, and the suburbs near Samsung's site in Taylor all see strong PNW demand. We cover the job-driven move in depth in our dedicated relocation guide.

How Seattle neighborhoods translate to Austin

  • Top schools and greenery: Westlake / Eanes ISD, Lake Travis, and Leander ISD areas.
  • Walkable and central (Capitol Hill, Ballard feel): Mueller, Clarksville, Travis Heights, East Austin.
  • Job-driven (Tesla, Apple, Oracle, Samsung): Northwest corridor, the Domain, Cedar Park, Taylor.
  • Water and outdoors: Lake Austin and Lake Travis areas, greenbelt-adjacent neighborhoods.

This is the change Seattle transplants feel most, and we would rather you plan for it than be surprised by it. In broad strokes, the two climates are close to a reversal of each other.

The honest framing we give clients: most PNW transplants are happy to trade months of gray drizzle for sunshine, and they fall in love with Austin's spring and fall. The adjustment is summer. From June through September the heat is real, and outdoor life shifts toward early mornings, the lakes, Barton Springs, and shaded greenbelt trails. Seattle's mountain access is something you will miss; Austin's lake and swimming-hole culture is what replaces it.

Honest Tradeoffs

No move is all upside, and we would rather you hear the caveats from us than learn them after closing.

Summer Heat Is a Real Adjustment

If long, cool summers were part of why you loved Seattle, Austin's heat is the biggest lifestyle change you will make. It is manageable, and locals adapt, but it is worth being clear-eyed about before you commit.

Property Taxes Offset Housing Savings

Texas funds itself through property taxes rather than income tax, so the rate is higher than what you paid in Washington. On a given home, that annual number belongs in your budget from day one, and it eats into the housing savings if you only look at purchase price. We always run the full monthly carrying cost.

No Mountains, Different Greenery

Austin is green and full of water, but it is Hill Country green, not Cascade evergreen. The dramatic mountain backdrop and the cool, lush PNW landscape are not here. Many people come to love the Hill Country on its own terms, but it is a genuinely different setting.

The Tax Story Is Smaller Than You Have Heard

Much of the Texas relocation narrative is built around escaping California income tax. As a Seattle mover you already had no state income tax, so do not over-weight that part of the pitch. The case for Austin is housing, cost of living, and growth, and it is strong enough without overstating the tax angle.

Frequently Asked Questions About Moving to Austin from Seattle

Is Austin cheaper than Seattle?

For most buyers, yes, Austin is meaningfully cheaper than Seattle on housing, which is the largest line item. Greater Austin's median home price runs roughly $450,000 to $550,000, while the Seattle metro median sits notably higher, often in the $750,000 to $850,000 range, and central Seattle neighborhoods run higher still. That gap means Seattle home equity stretches further here, whether you are buying more space, a newer build, or a shorter commute. Day to day costs like groceries and dining are broadly comparable. The honest caveat is property taxes: Texas property tax rates are higher than Washington's, so confirm the full monthly carrying cost for your specific home, not just the purchase price.

Where do Seattle transplants live in Austin?

Pacific Northwest buyers tend to cluster where greenery, walkability, and a short tech commute meet. Families chasing top schools land in Westlake (West Lake Hills) and the Eanes ISD area, or in Lake Travis and Leander ISD communities farther west. Buyers who want a Capitol Hill or Ballard feel gravitate to central and east Austin: Mueller, Clarksville, Travis Heights, and East Austin. Those relocating for jobs at Tesla, Apple, Oracle, or Samsung often choose the northwest and Domain corridor, Cedar Park, and the suburbs near Samsung in Taylor. We match the right pocket to your commute, schools, and lifestyle rather than guessing.

How is the weather different from Seattle?

It is close to a reversal. Seattle is mild, gray, and famously wet, with long stretches of overcast drizzle and cool summers. Austin is sunny most of the year, roughly 230 sunny days, with hot summers where highs frequently top 100 degrees from June through September. Winters in Austin are short and mild. The trade many PNW transplants make is gladly giving up months of gray skies for sunshine, in exchange for adapting to intense summer heat. Spring and fall in Austin are genuinely excellent. We are honest with clients: if the summer heat concerns you, it is a real adjustment worth planning around.

Does moving from Seattle to Austin save on taxes?

Less than people moving from California assume, because Washington already has no state income tax, just like Texas. So a Seattle to Austin move does not unlock the big income-tax windfall that a California or New York move does. The tax picture is closer to a wash on income, and Texas property taxes are higher than Washington's, which can offset some housing savings. The real financial case for Seattle professionals is housing cost, equity that buys more home, lower overall cost of living, and continued job and population growth, rather than income tax. We model the full carrying cost so the comparison is honest.
