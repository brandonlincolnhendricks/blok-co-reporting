Why Westhaven Is Franklin's Number One Most Searched Neighborhood

Westhaven sits on approximately 1,500 acres in western Franklin, roughly 3 miles from Historic Downtown. It was developed by Southern Land Company, which broke ground on the project in the early 2000s and first welcomed homeowners in 2003. The community is planned to reach approximately 3,500 single-family homes upon full build-out, with completion targeted around 2031. As of today, more than 2,400 households call Westhaven home.

What separates Westhaven from virtually every other Franklin neighborhood is its design philosophy. The community was built on the principles of new urbanism: walkable streets, mixed-use zones, front-porch architecture, and green space positioned every quarter mile to encourage foot traffic over car trips. There are no cul-de-sacs. Homes sit close to the sidewalk. Porches face the street. The village center is real and functional, not decorative.

For relocating buyers, particularly those arriving from Chicago, New York, Atlanta, or coastal markets, Westhaven represents the version of suburban living they actually want: the safety and space of the suburbs, combined with the walkability and social energy of a real neighborhood. That combination is genuinely rare in middle Tennessee, which is why the search volume for Westhaven outpaces every other Franklin community by a significant margin.

Home Styles and Architecture

Westhaven's architectural standards are some of the most stringent in Franklin. The community features Craftsman, Traditional, and Southern-influenced styles, with strong design guidelines around exterior materials, porch requirements, and street-facing facades. Brick, stone, and high-quality fiber cement siding are common. Lots vary from standard village lots with tight setbacks that encourage neighborhood interaction, to larger estate lots that offer more privacy and square footage.

Builders who have operated in Westhaven include Southern Land Company's own building program along with custom and semi-custom builders. The result is a neighborhood that looks curated rather than cookie-cutter. Streetscapes are intentional, landscaped, and cohesive in a way that maintains long-term property values.

Westhaven Amenities and Lifestyle

The Village Center: Shops, Restaurants, and Community Life

The heart of Westhaven is its walkable village center anchored along Front Street and Westhaven Boulevard. This is not a strip mall with a few token businesses. It is a functioning commercial district with a Publix grocery store, coffee shops, restaurants, hair salons, a dentist, bank branches, and professional services. Residents do not need to leave the neighborhood for everyday errands.

Restaurants in the village center include Puckett's, a beloved Tennessee institution known for live music and Southern cuisine; Coal Town Public House, a modern American grill with a full bar; Oscar's Taco Shop; Kokomo, a Caribbean-inspired dining spot; and Coal Town Pizza, featuring a coal-fired oven. The variety and quality of dining options within walking distance is one of the most frequently cited reasons buyers love living in Westhaven.

Community events are organized through the Residents Club and Westhaven's events calendar, including seasonal celebrations, farmers markets, outdoor concerts on The Great Lawn, and holiday programming. For buyers relocating from out of town, the social calendar accelerates the process of feeling settled.

Pool Complex and Fitness Facilities

Westhaven's aquatic facilities are genuinely resort-caliber. The community operates five swimming pools, anchored by a large resort-style pool with an 8,000-square-foot deck area, a 119-foot water slide, a lazy river, and a snack bar. Additional pools include a heated saltwater lap pool for adult swimmers, a zero-entry pool with spray features for young children, and a competition-lane pool at the Skube Swim Center with a poolside cafe and grill.

The 15,000-square-foot Residents Club contains a fully equipped fitness center, a movement studio for classes, an art center, and a community theater. These are not amenity-brochure bullet points. They are actively used facilities that support the daily routines of Westhaven residents.

Parks, Trails, and Green Spaces

Nine miles of nature trails wind through Westhaven's 1,500 acres, covering seven distinct routes that range from moderate to more challenging terrain. Trail access points are located throughout the neighborhood, with primary trailheads at Westhaven's Wild Elm and Grassmere corridors. The Harpeth River greenway provides additional outdoor access in the broader Franklin area.

Green space is distributed deliberately throughout the neighborhood, with parks and gathering areas positioned approximately every quarter mile. Westhaven Lake, Magli Green, and The Great Lawn serve as community gathering spaces for events and everyday recreation. Playgrounds are distributed across the neighborhood for families with young children.

Golf: Westhaven Golf Club

The Westhaven Golf Club is a private, 18-hole championship course designed by Arthur Hills, Steve Forrest and Associates. The course opened in 2009, plays to a par 72 at 7,000 yards from the championship tees, and was named one of the best new courses in 2010 by both Golf Week and Golf Digest. The core-golf design integrates naturally with the rolling hills and ridgelines of western Williamson County.

Golf membership is separate from HOA dues and carries its own initiation fee and annual costs. For serious golfers, having a private Arnold Hills-designed course within the community is a meaningful quality-of-life benefit that cannot be replicated in most Franklin neighborhoods.

Walkability: One of Franklin's Most Walkable Communities

Westhaven is consistently cited as one of the most walkable neighborhoods in all of Franklin, Tennessee. The community was explicitly designed to reduce car dependency for daily life. Streets connect rather than dead-end. The village center is a genuine destination. Golf carts are permitted on community roads, which many families use as a secondary mode of transportation within the neighborhood.

For buyers relocating from walkable urban environments, Westhaven offers a real alternative to the fully car-dependent suburban experience. You can walk to get groceries, walk your children to school at Pearre Creek Elementary, walk to dinner, and walk to the pool. That combination is rare in suburban middle Tennessee and is a core reason Westhaven commands the search volume it does.

Schools Serving Westhaven

Elementary, Middle, and High School Assignments

All public schools serving Westhaven are part of Williamson County Schools, which Niche ranks as the fourth-best school district in Tennessee and which Public School Review places in the top 1% of public school districts statewide. Math proficiency across the district runs at 64%, compared to a Tennessee statewide average of 34%. Reading proficiency is 69%, versus a 37% state average.

Pearre Creek Elementary sits within the Westhaven community itself, making it walkable from most residential sections. Independence High School holds an overall Niche grade of A and ranks among the top 25 public high schools in the state of Tennessee, with a 3.9-star average from parents and students. Some sections of Westhaven may be zoned for Franklin High School rather than Independence. Buyers should confirm their specific zoning with Williamson County Schools at the address level before purchasing.

Private School Alternatives Nearby

Families who prefer private education have strong options within a short drive of Westhaven. Battle Ground Academy (BGA), located at 336 Ernest Rice Lane in Franklin, is an independent college preparatory school serving grades K through 12 and holds an A rating on Niche. The Montessori School of Franklin, at 1325 West Main Street, serves pre-K through grade 6 and is Williamson County's longest-operating Montessori program. Franklin Christian Academy and Franklin Classical School are additional alternatives in the area.

Residents can access downtown Franklin's restaurants, boutiques, entertainment, and the Williamson County square without a significant time commitment. Frequently Asked Questions About Westhaven, Franklin TN

How much do single-family homes cost in Westhaven, Franklin TN?

Single-family home prices in Westhaven range from the upper $700,000s to over $5 million. The median sale price is approximately $1,393,000, based on recent sales data, with an average price per square foot of $485 to $495. Most transaction volume occurs in the $1 million to $1.5 million range, with 318 homes sold in the prior 12-month period.

What amenities does Westhaven offer residents?

Westhaven residents enjoy a 15,000-square-foot Residents Club with a fitness center, movement studio, art center, and theater. Outdoor amenities include five swimming pools with a resort-style lazy river and 119-foot waterslide, a heated saltwater lap pool, tennis and pickleball courts, and 9 miles of nature trails with 7 distinct routes. What schools serve the Westhaven neighborhood in Franklin?

Westhaven is served by Williamson County Schools, ranked the fourth-best school district in Tennessee by Niche and in the top 1% of districts statewide by Public School Review. Elementary students attend Pearre Creek Elementary School, located within the community, earning an A overall grade on Niche with 78% of students proficient in math. Is Westhaven walkable?

Yes. Westhaven is one of the most walkable neighborhoods in Franklin, TN. The community was designed on new urbanism principles, with pedestrian-friendly streets, 9 miles of walking and biking trails, and a village center within walking distance of most homes. Residents can walk to a grocery store, restaurants, coffee shops, fitness facilities, and professional services without using a car. Golf carts are also permitted on community streets.

Westhaven is a mixed-use master-planned community that includes single-family homes as its primary residential product.
