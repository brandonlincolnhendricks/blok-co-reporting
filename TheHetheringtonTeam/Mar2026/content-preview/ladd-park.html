Ladd Park: Where Franklin Meets the Harpeth River

Ladd Park sits at the intersection of two things Franklin buyers want most: a true sense of community and honest access to the outdoors. The community is located just east of Interstate 65 off Peytonsville Road, tucked into the rolling hills of southeast Franklin. Unlike neighborhoods where "nature access" means a pocket park between two cul-de-sacs, Ladd Park was deliberately platted around 240 acres of common green space connected by over 10 miles of trails, with the Harpeth River forming a natural western boundary.

The planning philosophy here is legible the moment you drive in. Wide sidewalks line every street. Pocket parks appear between subdivisions. The trail network links sub-neighborhoods to the river corridor, the pools, and the larger greenway system rather than dead-ending at a fence line. For families who want their kids to ride bikes to a pool or walk to the river on a Saturday morning without loading up a car, that design intention matters enormously.

The community is still growing. Builders including Boulevard Homes and The Jones Company continue to deliver new construction within Ladd Park, meaning buyers have a genuine choice between established resale homes with mature landscaping and brand-new builds with modern floor plans and energy-efficient systems.

Why Outdoor-Loving Families Choose Ladd Park

The Harpeth River is more than scenery here. The City of Franklin and the Harpeth Conservancy have invested in formal river access, and Ladd Park is home to one of the farthest upstream canoe and kayak launch points on the Harpeth River in Franklin. The river runs 15 miles through the city, and Ladd Park sits at the start of that stretch, giving paddlers a full-day float option downstream. The access point also serves as a fishing and wading spot when water levels are low, making it a genuine year-round amenity rather than a seasonal novelty.

Home Styles and Lot Sizes

Ladd Park is not a single-builder community, and that variety is one of its strengths. Square footage ranges from approximately 1,866 square feet in the more entry-level sections up to 6,821 square feet in larger custom builds. The sweet spot for most buyers falls between 2,500 and 3,500 square feet, particularly in the Highlands sub-neighborhood. Lot sizes vary considerably, from courtyard-style lots with low maintenance demands to premium river-view parcels that offer more breathing room and better sightlines into the greenway.

Architectural styles skew traditional and craftsman, with covered front porches, brick and stone exteriors, and the kind of streetscape that encourages neighbors to actually talk to each other. New construction from current builders continues to deliver open-concept layouts with primary suites on the main level, three-car garages, and outdoor living spaces designed to take advantage of the community's natural surroundings.

HOA Fees

HOA fees in Ladd Park are reported at approximately $75 per month, which residents consistently describe as reasonable given what the fee covers: maintenance of 240 acres of common space, two resort-style pools, the trail network, the canoe launch, the clubhouse, basketball court, playgrounds, and organized community programming. Fee structures and amounts can vary by sub-neighborhood and are subject to annual board review. We recommend verifying the current fee directly with the HOA before closing. The Ladd Park HOA is reachable at laddhoa.com or board@laddhoa.com.

Outdoor Living and Nature Access

Harpeth River Proximity

The Harpeth River forms a key boundary of the Ladd Park community, and the neighborhood's relationship to the river is more developed than most. The community includes over two miles of Harpeth River frontage, and the Ladd Park canoe access point is one of the farthest upstream launch sites on the Harpeth River within Franklin's city limits. The City of Franklin Parks Department has placed mile markers along the river to help paddlers navigate, and the access point feeds directly into the Harpeth River Blueway, a mapped paddling route that runs downstream through the city.

The access point also serves as a fishing and wading destination. When water levels are low, it is a popular spot for families to wade into the river without needing to drive anywhere. When levels are right for paddling, residents drop a canoe or kayak and float downstream toward additional city-managed access points. The launch was built with community volunteer involvement, a detail that speaks to how seriously Ladd Park residents take their connection to this resource.

Community Trails and Green Spaces

The trail network in Ladd Park covers more than 10 miles of walking and biking paths woven through 240 acres of common space. This is not a loop around a retention pond. The trails connect sub-neighborhoods to the river corridor, to the community pools, to the parks, and to the broader Harpeth River greenway system. Families use the trails daily, and we hear from buyers who have made them a non-negotiable part of their search criteria after a single visit.

The 240 acres of common space translates to preserved tree canopy, meadow sections, and natural buffers between the built portions of the community. It is why Ladd Park feels less dense than its home count might suggest, and why resale demand within the community tends to hold even when the broader market softens.

Parks and Recreation Facilities

Ladd Park's amenity package includes two resort-style pools, one of which is a junior Olympic pool. Alongside the pools, the community offers a clubhouse, multiple playgrounds, a basketball court, pavilions, and organized gathering spaces designed for community events. The combination of structured facilities and unstructured natural space gives families real flexibility in how they spend time outside.

Schools Serving Ladd Park

Ladd Park feeds into the Williamson County School District, which is consistently ranked among the top public school systems in Tennessee. The district serves over 41,900 students across 52 schools and posted an ACT composite score of 24.7 for the Class of 2023, well above the Tennessee state average of 19.0.

Children in Ladd Park are currently zoned for Oak View Elementary, Fred J. Page Middle School, and Fred J. Page High School. School zoning can shift over time, and we always recommend buyers confirm current assignments directly with Williamson County Schools before making a purchase decision.

Is Ladd Park Right for You?

Ladd Park is a strong fit if you:

  • Want direct Harpeth River access for kayaking, canoeing, fishing, or wading
  • Have children and value a top-tier school pipeline at every grade level
  • Work in Cool Springs or along the I-65 corridor and want a short commute
  • Want 10+ miles of trails and 240 acres of greenspace as your backyard
  • Are shopping in the $800K to $1.2M range and want strong long-term resale fundamentals
  • Prefer a lower HOA fee compared to similar Franklin master-planned communities

Ladd Park may not be the right fit if you:

  • Need walkable access to restaurants and retail without driving
  • Want an on-site golf club or country club lifestyle
  • Are searching under $800,000 in Franklin
