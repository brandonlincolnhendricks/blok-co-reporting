Real Estate in McKays Mill

Home Prices and Value Proposition

McKays Mill median sale prices tracked between $770,000 and $805,000 in 2025, with Redfin reporting a 4.5% year-over-year increase in June 2025. That figure reflects a robust, stable market rather than a speculative one. Importantly, the median does not tell the full story here because the community spans six distinct sections with meaningfully different price profiles.

The entry level of the market sits in the mid $400,000s, primarily represented by the Park Run section of the community, which features one-level condominiums. From there, pricing steps up through townhomes and standard single-family homes before reaching the executive tier, where homes in the Haddon Hall section can reach $700,000 and above. For buyers who want Franklin's location and school quality at a price point below what many other established neighborhoods require, McKays Mill is one of the clearest answers in the market.

Home Styles and Sizes

McKays Mill contains over 1,300 homes across six sections, offering genuine variety in housing type and size. The community includes single-family homes, townhomes, and condominiums. Square footage ranges from approximately 1,000 square feet in the condominium sections to over 7,000 square feet in the Haddon Hall executive section. A typical single-family home in McKays Mill runs between 2,500 and 4,500 square feet. Architectural styles trend toward traditional Southern and craftsman aesthetics, with front porches, brick and stone exteriors, and mature landscaping throughout the community.

HOA and Community Fees

The McKays Mill HOA fee is estimated at approximately $70 per month for most sections, though fees can vary by section and property type. The HOA maintains the community pool complex, clubhouse, fitness center, common green spaces, walking trails, and playground areas. The McKays Mill HOA has an official presence at 1215 Habersham Way, and the clubhouse is available to residents for private event rentals. We always recommend that buyers confirm the specific HOA fee and what it covers with their agent at the time of offer, as fees are subject to change and can differ between the community's six sections.

Amenities and Lifestyle

Pool Complex and Clubhouse

The centerpiece of McKays Mill's amenity package is its Olympic-size saltwater swimming pool, located on the south side of the 5,000-square-foot clubhouse. The pool area also includes a dedicated kiddie pool and a hot tub, making it a genuine gathering point for families during the warmer months. The clubhouse itself houses a fitness center and is available for private event rentals by residents. The pool complex is one of the most consistently praised features by McKays Mill homeowners, with residents highlighting the spacious layout and quality maintenance on review platforms and community forums.

Tennis, Playgrounds, and Walking Trails

Beyond the pool, McKays Mill offers a well-rounded outdoor amenity package. The community includes tennis courts, a half-basketball court, multiple playgrounds with age-appropriate equipment, a gazebo, and three separate walking and jogging trails winding through the community's green spaces. The trail network connects different sections of the neighborhood, making it practical for residents to walk or jog without repeating the same route. The community's 540 acres provide genuine green space rather than token landscaping, which is a distinguishing feature compared to newer, higher-density Franklin neighborhoods.

Community Events and Town Center

The McKays Mill HOA organizes community events throughout the year, with the summer months featuring bounce houses, barbecues, pool tournaments, and live entertainment. These events reinforce the neighborhood's strong sense of community identity, which residents consistently cite as one of McKays Mill's defining qualities.

What truly separates McKays Mill from most planned communities in the Franklin area is the McKays Mill Village Center, a built-in retail and services hub located at the heart of the neighborhood at 1400 Liberty Pike. The Village Center anchors Publix Supermarket (Store #1031), McKays Mill Dental, McKays Mill Animal Hospital, Mint Julep Hair Salon, childcare facilities at The Academy of McKays Mill, medical offices, and dining options including Mexican, pizza, and Chinese cuisine. Having a full-service grocery store and primary care services within walking distance of most homes is an operational reality that rarely comes with established Nashville-area neighborhoods at this price point.

Schools Serving McKays Mill

McKays Mill's school assignment depends on your specific address within the community. Homes are zoned for either Williamson County Schools (WCS) or the Franklin Special School District (FSSD), and both are among the highest-performing school systems in Tennessee. When you work with us, we always verify school zoning during the search process because both district assignments represent excellent outcomes, but they do lead to different schools.

Clovercroft Elementary is one of the standout schools in the entire Nashville area. Niche ranks it #2 in Best Public Elementary Schools in Tennessee and in the top 1% of elementary schools nationally. The school serves 680 students in grades K–5 with a 17:1 student-teacher ratio. State test scores show 94% of students proficient in math and 89% in reading.

Liberty Elementary (FSSD) holds a National Blue Ribbon School designation, a five-year recognition from the U.S. Department of Education covering 2021–2026. It ranks in the top 9.7% of Tennessee elementary schools.

Fred J. Page High School is shared by both districts and ranks #34 in Best Public High Schools in Tennessee (Niche 2025), with additional recognition among the top STEM and college prep high schools in the state.

Freedom Middle School (FSSD) has been designated a National Model PLC at Work school by Solution Tree, recognizing its implementation of professional learning communities and student support frameworks.

Location: Why Central Franklin Matters

Franklin, TN is a large city covering substantial geographic area, and where you sit within it shapes your daily experience significantly. McKays Mill is positioned in one of the most strategically central locations in the entire Franklin market, a point that we emphasize to relocating buyers who may not fully appreciate until they start driving the routes themselves.

Proximity to Cool Springs, Downtown Franklin, and I-65

McKays Mill sits approximately 2 miles east of Interstate 65 and Cool Springs Boulevard. That places the Cool Springs Galleria, anchor retailers, and the dense commercial corridor along Carothers Parkway within a 5 to 10 minute drive with no highway required. Cool Springs is also the location of many Nashville-area corporate campuses and employers, making McKays Mill a practical commute address for professionals working in that corridor.

Historic downtown Franklin is approximately 10 minutes north via Liberty Pike. That route stays off the highway for most of the drive, making it a reliable daily trip rather than a highway-dependent commute that degrades during peak hours.

Access to Interstate 65 takes roughly 5 to 7 minutes from most McKays Mill addresses. From I-65, downtown Nashville is approximately 20 to 25 minutes in normal traffic. Brentwood is even closer. The practical result is that McKays Mill residents can access virtually the entire southern Nashville metro within a 30-minute window on most days, which is a meaningful advantage over neighborhoods positioned further east or south in Williamson County.

Who Is McKays Mill Right For?

McKays Mill is an excellent fit for buyers who want to be in central Franklin without overpaying for a single-profile neighborhood. It is particularly well-suited for:

  • Families with school-age children who want access to either Williamson County Schools or FSSD without compromise
  • Professionals working in Cool Springs or on I-65 who want a short, predictable commute
  • Buyers entering the Franklin market who want strong resale fundamentals and community infrastructure already in place
  • Downsizers or empty nesters who want a condominium or smaller home in a full-amenity community without leaving Franklin
  • Relocating families who want one neighborhood that solves for schools, amenities, location, and community all at once

If your priority is new construction, a more rural feel, or a luxury price point above $1 million, McKays Mill may not be the right fit, and we can point you toward communities that serve those needs better.

Frequently Asked Questions About McKays Mill

What is the average home price in McKays Mill, Franklin TN?

Median sale prices in McKays Mill ranged from approximately $770,000 to $805,000 in 2025, depending on the month and data source. Active listings have varied more widely because the community includes everything from one-level condominiums starting in the $400,000s to executive single-family homes exceeding $700,000. Buyers should expect the typical single-family home in McKays Mill to fall between $550,000 and $750,000 as of early 2026.

Why is McKays Mill on every "best of Franklin" list?

McKays Mill appears on nearly every best neighborhoods list for Franklin, TN because it combines central location, resort-style amenities, diverse housing options, a built-in town center with a Publix grocery store and dining, and access to top-rated schools across two excellent districts. The 540-acre community offers something for families, young professionals, and downsizers alike, making it one of the most versatile planned communities in the greater Nashville area.

What schools serve McKays Mill?

McKays Mill is served by two highly rated school districts depending on your specific address. Homes zoned for Williamson County Schools attend Clovercroft Elementary (ranked top 1% nationally and #2 in Tennessee by Niche), Page Middle School, and Page High School. Homes zoned for the Franklin Special School District attend Liberty Elementary (a National Blue Ribbon School), Freedom Middle School, and Page High School. Both districts rank among the top in Tennessee, and Page High School serves all McKays Mill students at the secondary level.

What amenities are in McKays Mill?

McKays Mill offers an Olympic-size saltwater swimming pool with a kiddie pool area, a 5,000-square-foot clubhouse with a fitness center, tennis courts, a half-basketball court, multiple playgrounds, three walking and jogging trails, and a gazebo with green space. The community also features the McKays Mill Village Center with a Publix grocery store, dental office, animal hospital, hair salon, medical offices, childcare, and dining options, all within the neighborhood itself.

How close is McKays Mill to Cool Springs and downtown Franklin?

McKays Mill is located approximately 2 miles east of Interstate 65 and Cool Springs Boulevard, placing the Cool Springs Galleria, major retailers, and corporate offices within a 5 to 10 minute drive. Historic downtown Franklin is approximately 10 minutes away via Liberty Pike. The neighborhood sits just off Liberty Pike, which provides a direct, low-traffic route to both destinations without requiring highway driving.

Is McKays Mill a good value compared to other Franklin neighborhoods?

McKays Mill offers strong value relative to comparable Franklin neighborhoods. Fieldstone Farms carries a median price near $708,000. Berry Farms active listings averaged $820,700 in late 2025. McKays Mill median sale prices of $770,000 to $805,000 in 2025 reflect a community with more housing diversity, including entry points in the $400,000s for condominiums alongside executive homes exceeding $700,000 for single-family residences. For buyers who want central Franklin, top schools, and full resort amenities, McKays Mill consistently delivers competitive value.
