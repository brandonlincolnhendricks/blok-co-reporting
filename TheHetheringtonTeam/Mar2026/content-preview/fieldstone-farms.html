Fieldstone Farms — An Established Franklin Favorite

We have sold homes across Franklin for over 20 years, and Fieldstone Farms holds a place that is genuinely hard to replicate. This is one of Franklin's largest and most established master-planned communities, spanning 720 acres and 2,137 single-family homes built primarily between 1987 and the early 2000s. What sets it apart today is exactly what made it special then: mature oak canopies lining quiet streets, four community parks, neighbors who actually know each other, and a location that puts you minutes from everything Franklin offers without the premium price tag of newer, trendier developments.

Families consistently seek Fieldstone Farms out for the same reasons. The schools are exceptional. The amenity package rivals communities that charge twice the HOA dues. And the neighborhood has a stability that newer subdivisions take decades to build. When clients ask us where established Franklin families tend to put down long-term roots, Fieldstone Farms is almost always part of that conversation.

We also featured Franklin's best suburbs in our YouTube video "Top 5 Nashville Suburbs to Live in 2026" — Fieldstone Farms is the kind of community that makes that list for good reason.

Real Estate in Fieldstone Farms

Home Prices and Recent Sales

Fieldstone Farms has held its value well through the broader market normalization that Franklin saw in 2024 and into 2025. Based on data from Redfin and NeighborhoodScout, the median sale price sits in the $710,000–$735,000 range, with the most recent reported median of $734,000 representing a 12% year-over-year increase. Price per square foot averages around $279, up approximately 3.1% from the prior year. Listings are spending roughly 33–40 days on market, which is competitive for an established neighborhood with limited new inventory.

Entry-level homes in the community's smaller sections start in the mid-$600s, while larger homes on bigger lots in premium sections have transacted above $900,000. The NeighborhoodScout data places Fieldstone Farms in the top 6.8% of all Tennessee neighborhoods by real estate value, and in the top 20.3% nationally — a meaningful signal for buyers focused on long-term appreciation.

Home Styles — What to Expect

Fieldstone Farms is not a cookie-cutter neighborhood. Because development spanned roughly 15 years across 26 sub-associations within the master community, buyers find genuine variety in architecture and lot size. The predominant styles are traditional, colonial, and transitional single-family homes, with most ranging from 1,800 to 4,500+ square feet. Lots are generally generous by Franklin standards, giving homes a sense of space and privacy that newer high-density communities cannot match. Mature landscaping — established trees, manicured shrubs, well-kept lawns — gives the neighborhood a lived-in quality that photographs beautifully and feels even better in person.

HOA Fees and Community Costs

The Fieldstone Farms HOA is managed by Community Management Associates (CMA) and covers the full amenity package described below. Standard HOA dues average approximately $60 per month for most sections of the community. Some specialty sections, such as Windsor Park, carry higher dues (approximately $75/month for Fieldstone Farms dues plus $240/month for Windsor Park-specific services that include exterior maintenance and lawn care). For the amenity level provided, the base HOA fee is among the most competitive in Franklin.

What Makes Fieldstone Farms a Smart Buy

Three factors make this neighborhood stand out from a pure investment standpoint. First, supply is structurally limited — this is a fully built-out community with no new construction. When a home comes to market, it competes only against the existing resale pool, not a builder with a model home next door. Second, the school assignments are exceptional and strongly correlated with home values in Williamson County. Third, the central Franklin location means the neighborhood is insulated from commute-related demand shifts in ways that more peripheral suburbs are not. Buyers who prioritize long-term value stability consistently find Fieldstone Farms a compelling entry point into the Franklin market.

Amenities and Community Life

Pool, Tennis Courts, and Recreation Areas

The HOA operates two community pools: Club West Pool at 1530 Lexington Parkway and Club East Pool at 501 Black Horse Parkway. Both pools are maintained by the HOA and are included in your monthly dues. The tennis and pickleball program is equally robust: the community features two clay tennis courts and two hard tennis courts, with four pickleball courts layered onto the hard court surfaces. A clubhouse with a fitness center rounds out the active recreation package. This is a notably full amenity set for a neighborhood where HOA dues hover around $60/month.

Walking Trails and Green Spaces

One of the most commented-on features among Fieldstone Farms residents is the walking and biking trail network that winds through the community's mature tree canopy and landscaped greenways. There are four community parks, each equipped with playgrounds and connected by trails. Developers deliberately preserved the property's rural farmland character during construction, and those intentional green buffers translate directly into the neighborhood's feel today — it reads as a genuine park environment rather than an afterthought.

The trail system also provides rare walkable access to nearby shops and restaurants via Hillsboro Road, which is meaningful in a market where most Franklin neighborhoods require a car for every errand.

Community Events and Neighborhood Culture

Fieldstone Farms has an active HOA presence and a strong neighborhood identity. The community maintains its own website (myfieldstonefarms.com) and social channels, which are genuinely active — a reliable indicator of engaged residents rather than a passive association. Seasonal events, pool gatherings, and park programming contribute to the word-of-mouth reputation that has kept Fieldstone Farms on buyers' shortlists for over three decades. Residents consistently describe the neighborhood as family-friendly, dog-friendly, safe, and well-maintained in public review platforms.

Schools Serving Fieldstone Farms

Fieldstone Farms is zoned to Williamson County Schools, which Niche grades as an A-rated district and which ranks among the top school districts in Tennessee. In 2025, WCS students posted a class ACT composite of 25.3 — compared to the Tennessee state average of 19.3 — the best in the state. The district's math proficiency rate of 67% is nearly double the Tennessee public school average of 34%.

Location — Central Franklin Convenience

Fieldstone Farms sits on the eastern side of Franklin, positioned along Hillsboro Road (US-431), which is one of the most useful corridors in the market because it connects directly to downtown Franklin, Brentwood, Green Hills, and Nashville without requiring interstate access. This matters on a daily basis — residents can navigate most of their routine without ever merging onto I-65.

Which Buyers Is Fieldstone Farms Right For?

Fieldstone Farms is an excellent fit if you are:

  • A family prioritizing Williamson County school access with Hunters Bend Elementary on-site
  • A buyer who wants established character — mature trees, settled neighbors, long-term community identity — rather than a newly built neighborhood still finding its personality
  • Someone looking for the best amenity-to-HOA-cost ratio in Franklin (two pools, four tennis courts, fitness center, trails, all for ~$60/month)
  • A buyer with a budget in the $650,000–$850,000 range who wants Franklin's school district without Westhaven's $1M+ entry point
  • A remote worker or Cool Springs commuter who values being off the interstate corridor

Fieldstone Farms may not be the right fit if you:

  • Require new construction or the ability to customize a home from the ground up
  • Want on-site golf or a private club environment within the neighborhood
  • Are prioritizing walkability scores over community feel

Frequently Asked Questions About Fieldstone Farms

How much do homes cost in Fieldstone Farms, Franklin TN?

Homes in Fieldstone Farms, Franklin TN sell for a median price in the range of $720,000 to $734,000 based on 2024 and early 2025 market data from Redfin and NeighborhoodScout. The entry-level price point for the neighborhood starts in the mid-$600,000s, with larger or premium-section homes transacting above $900,000. The median price per square foot is approximately $279, up about 3.1% year over year. Fieldstone Farms ranks in the top 6.8% of all Tennessee neighborhoods by real estate value.

What amenities does Fieldstone Farms offer?

Fieldstone Farms offers two community swimming pools (Club West at 1530 Lexington Parkway and Club East at 501 Black Horse Parkway), two clay tennis courts, two hard tennis courts, four pickleball courts, a clubhouse with a fitness center, four community parks with playgrounds, and an extensive network of walking and biking trails throughout the 720-acre community. All amenities are maintained by the HOA and included in the monthly dues, which average approximately $60 per month.

What schools serve Fieldstone Farms?

Fieldstone Farms is zoned to Williamson County Schools, consistently ranked among the top school districts in Tennessee. The primary schools serving the neighborhood are Hunters Bend Elementary (K–5, located inside the neighborhood, rated top 5% in Tennessee), Grassland Middle School (6–8, top 5% in Tennessee), and Franklin High School (9–12, Niche grade A, ranked #13 in Tennessee). Williamson County Schools posted the highest ACT composite score in the state in 2025 at 25.3. School assignments should be verified directly with the district by specific address.

Is Fieldstone Farms a good neighborhood for families?

Fieldstone Farms is widely regarded as one of the best family neighborhoods in Franklin, Tennessee. The community features an elementary school (Hunters Bend Elementary) located within the neighborhood itself, four parks with playgrounds, walking trails, two pools, and active community programming through the HOA. Residents consistently describe the neighborhood as family-friendly, safe, dog-friendly, and well-maintained. The combination of top-ranked Williamson County schools, generous lot sizes, mature landscaping, and a fully built-out amenity package makes Fieldstone Farms a strong long-term choice for families.

How does Fieldstone Farms compare to McKays Mill?

Fieldstone Farms and McKays Mill are both established Franklin neighborhoods with Williamson County school access and HOA amenity packages, but they differ in several ways. Fieldstone Farms is larger (2,137 homes on 720 acres versus McKays Mill's approximately 1,500 homes on 540 acres) and slightly more affordable, with median prices in the $720,000–$734,000 range compared to McKays Mill's median closer to $800,000. Fieldstone Farms also has a more extensive tennis and pickleball court offering (four tennis plus four pickleball courts versus McKays Mill's tennis courts). McKays Mill features an Olympic-size pool and a 5,000-square-foot clubhouse and may appeal to buyers seeking a slightly smaller community feel. Both are excellent choices for families, and the right fit often comes down to specific home styles, lot preferences, and precise school zoning.

What year was Fieldstone Farms built?

Fieldstone Farms development began in 1987 when the land was acquired by Harlan Co. and construction of the neighborhood started. The 2,137 homes were built over approximately six years, with the primary development period running from the late 1980s through the mid-to-late 1990s, and some sections continuing into the early 2000s. The land itself has a much longer history: before development, it was a working farm known as Cecilwood, purchased by Cecil Sims in 1927. The name Fieldstone Farms comes directly from the fieldstones that dotted the original farmland. Today the neighborhood is fully built out, meaning all homes are resales with no new construction available within the community.
