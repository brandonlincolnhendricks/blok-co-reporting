Legends Ridge — Custom Estates on Large, Private Lots

Legends Ridge sits on the northwest edge of Franklin, south and west of the Harpeth River, in a part of Williamson County where mature hardwoods still dominate the landscape. The neighborhood was established in 1997 and developed through the mid-2010s, which means the trees are fully grown and the streetscape has the settled, rooted character that new construction communities simply cannot replicate.

The community contains approximately 200 homes spread across multiple sections. Because Legends Ridge was built by a variety of custom builders rather than a single production builder, no two homes look alike. You will find traditional brick and stone Colonials next to French Country estates next to craftsman-inflected custom builds, all on lots generous enough that neighbors feel comfortably distant. The gated entrance adds a layer of security and privacy that residents consistently cite as one of the neighborhood's top qualities.

Custom Builders and Architecture

Because Legends Ridge was developed over roughly two decades by multiple custom builders, architectural variety is one of the neighborhood's defining traits. You will find brick-and-stone traditional estates, French Country-inspired facades, and updated craftsman-influenced designs sitting side by side on large, wooded lots. Interior finishes vary just as widely, from classic millwork and coffered ceilings to fully renovated open-plan layouts with chef's kitchens and wine rooms.

This variety is meaningful for buyers. Unlike a community built by a single production builder, where every home follows one of a handful of floor plans, Legends Ridge allows you to find a home whose character genuinely matches what you are looking for. It also means that resale values are somewhat property-specific, making it especially important to work with an agent who knows the neighborhood intimately and can evaluate condition, lot position, and finish quality accurately.

HOA and Community Costs

Legends Ridge is managed by Ghertner and Company, one of the more reputable HOA management firms in the Nashville area. HOA dues vary by section within the community and range from approximately $235 to $807 per quarter. Those fees cover maintenance of all common areas, the community pool and clubhouse, the tennis courts, landscaping of shared green spaces, the stocked fishing lake, and walking trails. Gated access and private security are also included.

On the property tax side, Williamson County has one of the lowest effective tax rates in Tennessee, sitting around 0.54% to 0.56% of assessed market value. On a $2.5 million property, that equates to a roughly $13,500 to $14,000 annual tax bill, well below what buyers would pay on a comparable property in many other high-quality suburban markets nationally.

Amenities

Community Pool and Clubhouse

The Legends Ridge pool and clubhouse serve as the social hub of the community during warmer months. The facility is well-maintained and gives residents a resort-quality gathering space without leaving the neighborhood. The clubhouse can also be reserved for private events, making it a practical amenity for families who host frequently.

Tennis Courts

The community has two lighted tennis courts, which means play is not limited to daylight hours. Pickleball use has grown on the courts as the sport has expanded in popularity throughout Middle Tennessee. For buyers who are active players, the courts are a genuine draw and one of the features that places Legends Ridge above many comparably priced neighborhoods that offer only a pool.

Stocked Fishing Lake

The 3.5-acre fishing lake at the heart of Legends Ridge is genuinely one of the neighborhood's most distinctive features. Fully stocked and operated on a catch-and-release basis, the lake is surrounded by a lakeside playground and a party pavilion that residents use for gatherings throughout the year. The water views from lake-adjacent lots add meaningful value to specific properties within the community. For families with children, the combination of a fishing lake, playground, and pavilion creates a backyard that most neighborhoods simply cannot match.

Privacy and Natural Settings

The natural character of Legends Ridge deserves its own mention. The community encompasses more than 35 acres of professionally landscaped common areas, green spaces, and nature trails that wind through mature hardwood forests. When you add that to the fact that most homes sit on lots of nearly an acre or more, with meaningful tree coverage and setbacks, the sense of privacy here is genuinely different from what you find in Franklin's more densely developed luxury communities.

Bluff-view lots within the community offer elevated sightlines over the surrounding wooded hills, and the trail system allows residents to walk or jog within the neighborhood without ever touching a public road. For buyers coming from denser markets, or from neighborhoods where houses feel stacked on top of each other, Legends Ridge delivers a reset in how daily life feels.

Schools Serving Legends Ridge

Students in Legends Ridge attend schools in the Williamson County Schools district, which holds a Niche grade of A and ranks among the top 5 school districts in Tennessee. The district serves roughly 41,900 students across 52 schools with a student-to-teacher ratio of 15 to 1. According to state assessments, 64% of Williamson County students are proficient in math and 69% in reading, both well above Tennessee state averages.

Frequently Asked Questions About Legends Ridge

How much do estate homes in Legends Ridge, Franklin TN cost?

What amenities does Legends Ridge offer?

Legends Ridge features a gated entrance, a community swimming pool and clubhouse, two lighted tennis courts, a 3.5-acre stocked catch-and-release fishing lake with a lakeside playground and party pavilion, and miles of walking and nature trails through more than 35 acres of professionally landscaped common areas with mature hardwood forests. Does Legends Ridge have a fishing lake?

Lake-adjacent lots within the neighborhood command a premium due to the water views and the sense of open space they provide.

How large are the lots in Legends Ridge?

Lot sizes in Legends Ridge generally range from approximately 0.9 acres to more than 2 acres, with some outlier parcels exceeding that range. What schools serve Legends Ridge in Franklin TN?
