Gaps as opportunities

The July Plan – Index the Pillar, Then Widen the Wins

The wins are real and concentrated in your neighborhood guides. The gaps are specific, and each one is a concrete July task.

1. Get the Living in Franklin pillar indexed

The pillar earns zero citations on every engine because it is not yet in Google’s index. It cannot be cited until it is indexed, and it feeds internal links to every neighborhood guide. Requesting indexing and adding internal links is the single highest-leverage move this month.

2. Win the Historic Downtown question

Your downtown guide is cited on the walkable-neighborhood phrasing on ChatGPT but not on “Historic Downtown Franklin living” anywhere. The content is live; the fix is on-page emphasis and headings that match the historic-downtown intent.

3. Earn the ChatGPT relocation citation

Perplexity and Google both cite your Moving to Nashville relocation guide, but ChatGPT does not yet. A California-origin feeder post and a freshness pass are the levers to close a gap where the content already proves it can be cited.

4. Push the guides into the Google Overviews

On eight questions Google built an Overview without citing you, while citing competitors. Cool Springs, Franklin luxury, and the relocation guide already broke through; applying the same depth and structure to Westhaven and the downtown guides is how you widen that column.
